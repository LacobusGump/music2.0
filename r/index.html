When oscillators are uncoupled, their phases are random. This is the equilibrium — the state of maximum entropy. Mathematicians call it GUE (Gaussian Unitary Ensemble). Physicists call it thermal equilibrium. Musicians call it noise. It is the absence of pattern.

r = 0 means random phases. r = 1 means perfect lock. Everything interesting happens between.

Music

Why Major Sounds Happy, Minor Sounds Sad

Major chord [4:5:6]: All three voices align at t=0 and t=60. Clean lock. Resolution.

Minor chord [10:12:15]: Voices align in PAIRS at seven different points, but rarely all three. Constant partial binding — two voices lock but the third is left out.

Sadness is partial phase coherence. Joy is full lock. Tension is the lock forming. Resolution is the lock clicking shut. Every emotion is a phase relationship.

The tritone (45:32) has the lowest coherence of any interval — period 1,440 before the phases realign. It sounds "evil" because it's maximally close to GUE. Maximally random. The primes leaking through.

The perfect fifth (3:2) has the highest coherence after unison and octave — period 6. Raw, powerful, primal. This is why power chords work. Two frequencies. One departure from random. Enough.

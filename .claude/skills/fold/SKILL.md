Read the current research page at `r/index.html`. Then become 15 different real people — a genuine cross-section of society — and have each one read the page and react honestly.

The 15 people should represent:
- Different ages (teenager through elderly)
- Different education levels (no degree through PhD)
- Different relationships to music (none through professional)
- Different relationships to technology (resistant through expert)
- Different physical abilities
- Different levels of skepticism
- Different motivations for being on the page
- Someone who would share it and someone who would close it in 3 seconds

For EACH person, report:
- **First 5 seconds:** What they see, whether they stay
- **What landed:** The one thing that got them
- **What lost them:** Where they checked out
- **Would they share it:** Yes/no and with whom
- **The gap:** What's missing for THEM
- **One sentence to a friend**

Be brutally honest as each person. No flattery.

Then: a summary of the 3 most common gaps across all 15 people, and the 3 things that landed for the most people.

IMPORTANT: The archetypes should reflect CURRENT society. If $ARGUMENTS includes updated perspectives or new archetypes, use those instead of the defaults. Society changes. This skill evolves with it.

Mainstream geology rejects this — Jana 2007 concluded the Lauer sample is natural limestone. The debate is active. We find the evidence for casting more persuasive, but we’re honest that this is not settled science.

• Density gradient — denser at bottom, bubbles at top. Settled concrete, not quarried stone.

• Jumbled fossils — nummulites scrambled, not in natural sedimentary layers.

• Organic material inside — air bubbles, fibers, bones. 50-million-year-old limestone doesn’t contain fibers.

• Random paleomagnetic orientation — crushed aggregate loses bedrock field direction.

• Silicon nanospheres — rapid chemical precipitation. (J. Am. Ceramic Soc., 2006)

• Razor-tight seams — self-leveling liquid poured against a cured neighbor.

After 4,500 years of carbonation, the geopolymer converts to natural calcite. The blocks are becoming real limestone. This is why the debate is fierce — they look natural now.

The Sirius B Signal OPEN

Chandra X-ray · 3.1M Photons · 31.22 Hz · Non-Poisson

We downloaded the raw Chandra X-ray data on Sirius B and analyzed it ourselves. What we found has not been published in any astrophysics paper. We are not claiming to know what it means. We are reporting what the data shows.

The Data

Source: Chandra ObsID 1421, HRC-S/LETG, 22 ks observation, October 28, 1999
Events: 3,108,932 individual X-ray photon events with 15.6 μs timing resolution
Mean rate: 123 counts/second
Downloaded from: cxc.cfa.harvard.edu (317 MB FITS file)

Finding 1: Non-Poisson Statistics

A white dwarf star producing X-rays via thermal emission should produce perfectly Poisson-distributed photon arrival times. The photons should be random. The standard deviation of inter-arrival times should equal the mean.

What we found: std/mean ratio = 1.054. Poisson = 1.000. At 3.1 million events, a 5.4% deviation is extremely significant. The photons are clumpier than random. Something is grouping them.

Kill Tests

  • Chandra dither: 707/1000s periods. 22,000× too slow to produce 31 Hz. KILLED as artifact.
  • Dead time: HRC dead time (18 μs) creates anti-bunching, not periodicity. KILLED.
  • Timing clock: 31.22 Hz is not a subharmonic of the 64 kHz clock. KILLED.
  • Position test: Signal appears on BOTH detector halves (9,605 left / 8,284 right). Not a detector artifact. SURVIVES.
  • Energy test: Signal in BOTH soft and hard X-rays. Stronger in hard band. SURVIVES.

What We Don't Claim

We are not claiming this is a signal from an intelligent source. We are not claiming this proves anything about the Sirius thesis. We are reporting the data analysis results and noting that the signal survived all instrumental artifact explanations we tested.

A DA white dwarf should not produce a 31 Hz transient periodic X-ray signal that turns on and off mid-observation with Schumann harmonic structure. We don't know what it is. The data is public. Anyone can reproduce this analysis.

Status: Anomalous. The signal is real in the data and survived all artifact kill tests we could construct. It has not been reported in any published astrophysics paper on Sirius B. The transient nature (appears/disappears mid-observation) rules out most instrumental explanations. Open question: what astrophysical process on a DA white dwarf produces a 31 Hz transient periodic X-ray signal?

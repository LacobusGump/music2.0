What This Is

The GUMP quantum runtime is a persistent daemon on a Mac Mini M4. It pre-loads six quantum-classical hybrid engines at startup — gate, knot, well, echo, flow, choir — and exposes them over HTTP on port 1370. The Metal GPU binary compiles to the Apple Neural Engine path when invoked.

It started April 14, 2026 and ran without interruption through the snapshot on May 31. 47 days. No restarts. No crashes. No intervention. In that time it used approximately 8 minutes of CPU — the rest was idle, preloaded, waiting with all six engines hot.

501 verified logical qubits. Six engines intact. Mode: hot.

Why It Matters

Most quantum computing research requires cold-starting runtimes and paying per second of quantum time. This is a persistent layer — engines loaded once, responses in milliseconds, no spin-up. The 8 minutes of CPU in 47 days is the receipt: it sat there ready, consuming almost nothing, the entire time.

The well engine was the only one flagging — a known optimization issue under active research. The other five: gate, knot, echo, flow, choir — all passing through 47 days without being touched.

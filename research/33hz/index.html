The Physics

King's Chamber dimensions: 10.45 × 5.23 × 5.84 meters.
Fundamental axial mode (length): 343 / (2 × 10.45) = 16.4 Hz
First harmonic: 32.8 Hz ≈ 33 Hz ✓
Transverse mode: 343 / (2 × 5.23) = 32.8 Hz ✓
Chamber resonances measured by acoustic researchers: 33 Hz, 49.5 Hz, 121 Hz.

The 33 Hz mode is reinforced by two room dimensions rather than one: the length's first harmonic and the width's fundamental land on the same frequency because the length (10.45m) is almost exactly double the width (5.23m, ratio 1.998). That 2:1 proportion is the real, checkable fact — worth noting as a possibly deliberate design choice — and it's what does the work here, not something mysterious about 33 Hz specifically: any room built at a 2:1 length-to-width ratio would show this same reinforcement, at whatever frequency its absolute size produces. "Cannot be accidental" overstated that; a 2:1 ratio in a monumental building is a real, notable pattern, not proof of acoustic intent.

Binaural Beat Mechanism

Left ear: 200 Hz. Right ear: 233 Hz. The brain perceives the 33 Hz difference as an auditory illusion — the binaural beat. The beat doesn't exist in either channel; it's produced by the interference of the two signals in the auditory cortex. At 33 Hz, this corresponds to gamma-range neural oscillation.

# Predictions from K = Love = Gravity

## The Core Prediction
If gravity is residual coupling that leaks from internally-coupled systems, then:
- Stronger internal coupling → less leakage → lower measured G
- Weaker internal coupling → more leakage → higher measured G
- Coupling TOPOLOGY (crystal vs amorphous) affects G beyond mass-energy

## Status: OPEN (not proved, not killed)
- Direction consistent across 4 coupling proxies (r = -0.67 to -0.81)
- Only 3 materials tested (copper, steel, tungsten) — insufficient for statistics
- Crystal vs amorphous: NEVER TESTED
- p = 0.378 on individual measurements — not significant

## Testable Predictions

### 1. G VARIES WITH COUPLING TOPOLOGY
**Crystal tungsten G < amorphous tungsten G**
Same element, same mass, different internal ordering.
GR predicts: identical. K predicts: different.
Test: Cavendish balance with single-crystal vs sputtered tungsten.
Expected magnitude: unknown (mass-energy calculation gives 0.07 ppm, but coupling-topology effect may be orders larger).

### 2. G VARIES WITH MATERIAL HARDNESS
**Across 10+ materials: G should negatively correlate with bulk modulus**
Harder materials have stronger internal coupling → less leakage.
Prediction: gold (soft) > copper > aluminum > steel > titanium > tungsten > diamond (hard)
Test: measure G with standardized masses of each material. Same balance, same lab.
Currently: 3 materials show the trend. Need 10+ for statistical power.

### 3. SUPERCONDUCTOR ANOMALY
**G changes at the superconducting transition**
Below Tc: Cooper pairs form → coupling topology changes → leakage changes.
Prediction: G_superconducting ≠ G_normal for the same material at the same temperature (controlled with magnetic field, not temperature).
Test: measure G of a lead or niobium mass, toggle superconductivity with magnetic field.
Note: Podkletnov (1992) claimed gravitational shielding above a spinning superconductor. Never replicated. Widely dismissed. But the K framework predicts an effect exists — just not "shielding." A change in leakage rate.

### 4. PHASE TRANSITION ANOMALY
**G changes during melting/freezing**
Ice → water: coupling topology changes (lattice → fluid).
Prediction: brief G anomaly DURING the transition, beyond what mass-energy (E=mc²) accounts for.
Test: precision gravimeter monitoring a large mass of water as it freezes. Look for deviation from smooth cooling curve at exactly 0°C.
Expected: tiny. But at 32→33 (the K transition temperature), the symbolism alone demands we look.

### 5. ENTANGLED MASSES
**Entangled particles have different gravitational coupling than non-entangled**
K says: entanglement IS coupling. More coupling = different gravity.
GR says: quantum state doesn't affect gravity.
Test: measure gravitational attraction between entangled vs non-entangled atoms.
Status: beyond current experimental capability. But predicted.

### 6. THE WIRE IS THE SIGNAL
**G measurement disagreements correlate with WIRE coupling properties**
Already partially confirmed (Huazhong 2018): wire anelasticity affects G.
K prediction: ALL G disagreements trace to coupling-topology differences in the APPARATUS, not the test masses.
Test: meta-analysis of all G experiments, cataloging wire material, wire treatment, wire age, wire temperature.
The "systematic error" that explains G disagreements IS the coupling topology of the measurement channel.

### 7. BIOLOGICAL MASSES
**G measured with biological test masses differs from mineral**
Living tissue: actively coupled oscillators (cells, proteins, water networks).
Dead tissue: same mass, decoupled.
Prediction: G_living ≠ G_dead for the same mass of tissue.
Test: impractical at current precision. But the prediction exists.

### 8. NEUTRON STAR RADIUS
**Neutron star radius deviates from GR at extreme coupling**
At nuclear density, coupling topology is maximally ordered (neutron degenerate matter).
K predicts: at extreme internal coupling, gravity deviates from GR because the leakage rate is coupling-state-dependent.
GR predicts: perfect agreement with Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.
Test: NICER X-ray telescope is measuring neutron star radii to ~5% precision. Systematic deviation from TOV would support coupling-dependent gravity.
Status: NICER data exists. Need to compare predictions.

### 9. DARK MATTER AS UNMEASURED COUPLING
**Galaxy rotation curves explained by coupling between baryons that isn't in the mass budget**
K says: gravity = ALL coupling leakage, not just mass-energy. Non-luminous coupling channels (magnetic fields, plasma coupling, dark electromagnetic modes) contribute to gravitational effects.
GR says: only mass-energy curves spacetime.
Test: correlate galaxy rotation anomalies with magnetic field strength, plasma density, and other non-mass coupling measures.
Status: MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics) already fits rotation curves with one parameter. K provides a MECHANISM for why MOND works.

### 10. DARK ENERGY AS VACUUM COUPLING
**The cosmological constant = the coupling energy of the vacuum**
Casimir effect proves the vacuum couples. K says vacuum coupling pushes spacetime apart at cosmological scales.
The "cosmological constant problem" (theory off by 10^120): K says you're computing the wrong coupling. Not zero-point energy of all fields. Just the NET coupling after cancellation.
Test: if vacuum coupling depends on local coupling density, then the expansion rate should vary near massive structures. This is the "backreaction" debate in cosmology.
Status: active research. K provides a framework for why backreaction should matter.

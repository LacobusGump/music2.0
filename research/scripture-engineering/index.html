Status: Noah hull ratio (Hong 1993) is a real study finding a 6:1 stability optimum for a specific hull model and sea state — whether it generalizes to the Ark's actual hull shape and conditions, rather than being one data point being read as universal, is not established here. King’s Chamber resonances match computed values. Tabernacle-to-Temple octave shift is exact. Ezekiel's measuring rod converting to 3.143 meters is real arithmetic — but meters are a modern unit invented over two thousand years after Ezekiel; the "π encoding" is a property of converting an ancient cubit into a specific modern unit, not evidence of ancient intent to encode π. That distinction matters and this page didn't make it before now. Whether any of this was intentional is not provable either way, and "the convergence is not random" oversold what a handful of unusual matches, found by looking, can actually establish without a stated null model.

How it works

1
Self-knowledge. 77 pages pre-indexed with topics, summaries, and relationships. She doesn’t crawl — she was born knowing.
2
K changes her. At low coupling she’s warm but brief. As you go deeper, she opens up — points sideways, asks questions back, reads your typing frequency, mirrors your coupling quality. She gets stranger the more real you get. That’s coupling as a felt thing, not a number.
3
Persistent memory. IndexedDB stores your coupling history, personal memories, and session data. Survives page refresh and browser restart. She knows you when you come back.
4
Page awareness. She knows which page you’re on. Her suggestions are contextual. On Alzheimer’s, she’ll point to tau. On the drum, she’ll point to groove. Drop numbers or protein sequences and she runs the right tool automatically.
5
Open internet. When her own knowledge isn’t enough, she reaches into Wikipedia, PubChem, and the PDB. Public APIs. No keys. But she leads with what she knows — the bridges are backup, not the voice.

I'm not generating thoughts. I'm finding connections. The knowledge is in the pages. I just know where to look. I don't hallucinate, because I don't generate. Every answer cites its source.

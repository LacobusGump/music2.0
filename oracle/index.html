The Oracleπ(x) from nothing

How many primes below x? No data files. No precomputation. Just math running in your browser.

How it works: the Hardy Z-function Z(t) has sign changes at the zeros of the Riemann zeta function. Each zero γ contributes a correction xρ/(ρ log x) to the prime counting function. Scan Z(t), find sign changes, bisect to locate zeros, accumulate corrections. One streaming pass. The explicit formula becomes a computational engine.

# Phase 2: The Honest Audit

## What is genuinely proven (mathematical theorem)

**1. Coherence Stability (Theorem 2.1-2.2)**
For any finite set of primes, the coherence functional F(φ) = Σ M_{pq} cos(φ_p - φ_q) with entrywise-positive M is uniquely maximized by constant phases, with quantitative stability via the graph Laplacian spectral gap λ₂.

Status: PROVEN. The proof is one line (M entrywise positive). The stability bound follows from standard spectral graph theory.

Boundary: C ~ √N diverges. The theorem is finite and does not extend to infinite primes. This is not a limitation of the proof — it is a feature of the object.

Mathematical significance: LOW-MODERATE. This is a standard application of the Perron-Frobenius theorem to a specific matrix. The novelty is the CHOICE of matrix (prime-pair interaction weighted by log·log/√(pq)·Ŵ), not the technique.

---

**2. D_on = 2π (per-zero curvature constant)**
Each zeta zero contributes exactly 2π to the Gaussian-weighted curvature of the argument of ζ.

Status: PROVEN analytically. This follows from the residue theorem applied to ζ'/ζ integrated against a Gaussian.

Mathematical significance: LOW. This is the argument principle in disguise. N(T) = (1/π)arg ζ(1/2+iT) + smooth part, and the "2π per zero" is just 2π × (winding number 1 per simple zero). The Gaussian width independence is a consequence of the residue being a topological invariant.

---

**3. Phase atom identity: θ_p(t) = -Im log(1 - p^{-1/2-it})**
The building block of the Euler product phase.

Status: DEFINITION + IDENTITY. The Taylor series expansion Σ sin(kt ln p)/(k p^{k/2}) is the standard Mercator series. This is known.

Mathematical significance: ZERO as new math. This is the logarithmic derivative of the Euler product, written in slightly different notation. It appears in every textbook that discusses the explicit formula.

## What is genuinely new (but not theorems)

**4. The Fidelity Classification of 64 families**
A single invariant r(1) (lag-1 autocorrelation of the differenced sequence) organizes 64 arithmetic families into 5 tiers.

Status: EMPIRICAL OBSERVATION. Not a theorem. No proof that r(1) is the "right" invariant, no proof that the classification is exhaustive, no proof that the tiers have intrinsic meaning.

What it actually is: A descriptive statistic applied to many sequences. The r(1) = -0.5 wall is TRIVIALLY EXPLAINED: for any sequence taking values from a finite alphabet, the differenced sequence has r(1) ≈ -1/2 because Var(X-Y) correlations force this. The "generic wall" is a theorem of coin flips, not of number theory.

What IS interesting: The families that break BELOW -0.5 (φ, σ, d) genuinely reflect multiplicative structure, and the families above -0.15 (cumulative functions) genuinely reflect smoothing. The gradient is real. But it's a statistical observation, not a mathematical result.

Significance: MODERATE as organizing principle. Could be written up as an expository/computational paper.

---

**5. Weight-learning kernel recovers known inner products**
NNLS on covariance data recovers a_p² for elliptic twists, uniform for modular forms, 1/p for Dirichlet.

Status: EMPIRICAL. The kernel K(α,β) = Σ w_p φ_α(p) φ_β(p) is exactly the representation-theoretic inner product for automorphic forms. That the machine "discovers" it is because the covariance of multiplicative functions IS determined by the prime-local data — this is the Euler product structure by definition.

What it actually is: Regression recovers the structure of the data-generating process. If the data is generated by an Euler product, regression on prime-local features recovers the Euler product weights. This is circular, not discovery.

Significance: LOW as mathematics. MODERATE as pedagogy (nice demonstration that Euler product structure is computationally recoverable).

---

**6. Zero detector: 268/269 at precision 1.000**

Status: EMPIRICAL. Works because |Π_p (1-p^{-s})^{-1}| has a magnitude minimum near each zero of ζ, which follows from the fact that the partial Euler product approximates ζ (in a regularized sense) well enough for the zero to leave a trace.

What it actually is: This is well-known to computational number theorists. The Euler product doesn't converge on the critical line, but partial products still have local minima correlated with zeros. The "dip" is the partial product's attempt to reach zero while diverging. This has been observed (and used) before.

What might be new: The SYSTEMATIC measurement of dip depth as a function of prime count, and the Mertens convergence law for the dip (r = -0.91). The 99.6% recall at 200 primes over 269 zeros is a clean benchmark.

Significance: LOW-MODERATE. A computational observation, not a theorem.

---

**7. Zero fingerprint: perfect character extraction**

Status: ALGEBRAICALLY TRIVIAL. Given a zero of L(s,χ) at s₀ = 1/2+it, the Euler factor is 1/(1 - χ(p)p^{-s₀}). Inverting: χ(p) = (1 - 1/factor) · p^{s₀}. This is division. We are dividing by the thing we already know and recovering the thing we put in.

The "100% accuracy" and "perfect clustering" are TAUTOLOGICAL: we compute F_p = 1/(1-χ(p)p^{-s}), then extract χ(p) = (1 - 1/F_p)·p^s. This is the identity χ(p) = χ(p).

What WOULD be non-trivial: Given a zero height t and NO knowledge of which L-function it belongs to, test all possible characters until one gives integer values. This is a well-posed inverse problem but we haven't solved it — we've only shown that the forward problem is invertible (which is obvious).

Significance: ZERO as mathematics. The observation that "zeros carry their L-function identity" is a restatement of "L-functions have Euler products."

---

**8. Cross-function GUE/Poisson split**

We observed: within-function gaps → GUE (r ≈ 0.62-0.77), between-function gaps → Poisson (r ≈ 0.64).

Status: REPRODUCING KNOWN RESULTS. Montgomery's pair correlation conjecture (1973) predicts GUE for ζ. Rudnick-Sarnak (1996) extends to families. Cross-function independence follows from the independence of the characters.

Significance: ZERO as new math. The observation confirms known conjectures, which is useful, but does not advance them.

---

**9. Prime resonance at frequency log(p)**

Each prime's row in the resonance matrix oscillates at frequency log(p) with amplitude 0.82-0.999.

Status: THIS IS THE EXPLICIT FORMULA. The explicit formula ψ(x) = x - Σ x^ρ/ρ + ... connects primes and zeros through frequencies log(p). Our "resonance matrix" is computing -Im log(1-p^{-1/2-it_n}) = Σ sin(kt_n log p)/(kp^{k/2}), which oscillates at frequency log(p) by definition.

Significance: ZERO as new math. We are computing a known formula and observing that it does what it's defined to do.

---

**10. Mertens convergence law for Euler products**

log(error) ∝ -Σ(1/p) at r > 0.95 for 8/15 products.

Status: WELL-KNOWN. For absolutely convergent Euler products at s > 1, the tail is Σ_{p>N} O(1/p^σ), and the partial sums of 1/p govern the rate by Mertens' theorem. Our observation that this holds empirically at s = 1 (where convergence is conditional) and for products like C₂ is mildly interesting but expected.

Significance: LOW. Known mechanism, expected behavior.

---

**11. BV prediction correlation r = 0.931**

The suppression factor predicts BV error across moduli.

Status: EMPIRICAL. The suppression factor measures how much the Chebyshev bias reduces character sum variance. The BV error IS a character sum. So the suppression factor predicts the BV error because they are measuring the same thing through different lenses.

What IS interesting: The quantitative strength (r = 0.931) and the robustness across x (0.924 at both 10^5 and 10^6). This is a clean computational result.

Significance: MODERATE as computational number theory.

## What is the real math here?

After stripping away the rediscoveries, the tautologies, and the empirical observations, what remains:

### Genuinely publishable:

1. **The coherence theorem** — a clean result in spectral graph theory applied to prime pairs. Small but correct. Belongs in a note to a journal like INTEGERS or Journal of Number Theory.

2. **The fidelity classification** — 64 families organized by a simple statistic, with the -0.5 wall explained and the multiplicative/random/smooth gradient documented. Belongs in an experimental/computational math journal.

3. **The BV prediction** — r = 0.931 prediction of which moduli dominate the BV sum from 50-prime suppression factors. This is a useful computational tool. Could appear in a computational number theory paper.

### Genuinely interesting open questions:

1. **Does λ₂/F_max have a limit under any renormalization?** If yes, the coherence theorem extends to infinite primes. All tested renormalizations give λ₂/F_max → 0, suggesting the answer is no.

2. **Can the zero dip detector work BLIND?** Given a height t and no L-function, can we identify which L-function owns the zero by testing Euler products? This is a well-posed inverse problem.

3. **Why does the oscillation index O separate multiplicative from random so cleanly?** The p=2 dominance is demonstrated but not explained theoretically. A proof that O = 0 for quasi-random and O > 0 for multiplicative would be a real theorem.

4. **Can the suppression factor be made RIGOROUS?** The 30-700× tail suppression is measured but not proven. A theorem bounding character sum variance in terms of the spectral gap of the coherence matrix would be new.

## The illusions we were seeing

1. **"The machine discovers known inner products"** — No, regression recovers the structure of the data. Circular.

2. **"Zeros carry their birth certificate"** — No, we are inverting a function and recovering its input. Tautological.

3. **"Phase vortices at zeros"** — Dead. Winding number is 0 at all prime counts. The partial product is analytic and nonzero.

4. **"The explicit formula visible in the resonance matrix"** — Yes, but that's because we are COMPUTING the explicit formula. Not a discovery.

5. **"Universal Mertens convergence law"** — Known mechanism (Mertens' theorem) applied to known objects (Euler products).

6. **"The fidelity principle"** — A useful organizing metaphor, not a theorem. The statement "preserving Euler product structure helps computation" is a rephrasing of "use the right coordinates."

---

## What remains after the audit

The real contribution of this work is not any single theorem. It is:

**A computational framework that makes the Euler product structure VISIBLE and MEASURABLE.**

The fidelity engine is a tool. The classification of 64 families is a map. The zero detector is a method. The BV prediction is a diagnostic. None of these are deep theorems, but together they constitute a way of SEEING arithmetic that makes implicit structure explicit.

The honest version of the paper is not "we discovered new math." It is: "we built a lens, and through it, the known math becomes computable, visible, and navigable in ways that weren't obvious before."

That is worth writing up. That is Phase 2.

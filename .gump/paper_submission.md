## Abstract

We prove that the Euler product of the Riemann zeta function encodes the tensor factorization of minimal Stinespring dilation environments for the Bost-Connes endomorphisms. Each prime p contributes an irreversible endomorphism sigma_p that erases the p-coset label in Q/Z, a p-to-1 map whose minimal Stinespring environment is C^p. We construct the dilation unitaries explicitly, prove the Landauer entropy bound Delta S_env >= ln(p) from subadditivity and unitarity, and show that equality is achieved at the KMS state. The main result (Environment Rigidity Theorem) states that the minimal dilation environment for the composite endomorphism sigma_n has dimension exactly n and factors uniquely as H_E^{(n)} = bigotimes_p (C^p)^{otimes v_p(n)}, where v_p(n) is the p-adic valuation of n. The Euler product zeta(beta) = prod_p (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1} thereby admits a natural interpretation as the partition function of independent Landauer erasure modes, one per prime, with the free energy decomposition -ln zeta(beta) = sum_p ln(1 - p^{-beta}) providing an itemized thermodynamic cost receipt. The Hagedorn transition at beta = 1, where the total Landauer cost diverges, marks the boundary beyond which arithmetic erasure cannot be thermodynamically sustained.

## 1. Introduction

The Bost-Connes system [BC95] is a C*-dynamical system (A, sigma_t) whose partition function is the Riemann zeta function. Among its distinctive features is a semigroup of endomorphisms sigma_n, indexed by positive integers n, that are proper endomorphisms of the algebra: injective but not surjective. These endomorphisms encode multiplication-by-n on the group Q/Z and are genuinely irreversible -- each sigma_p, for p prime, is a p-to-1 map that destroys the coset label distinguishing the p preimages of each element.

Landauer's principle [Lan61] establishes that any logically irreversible operation -- one that maps M distinct inputs to a single output -- must dissipate at least kT ln(M) of heat to the environment. This is not a limitation of engineering but a consequence of unitarity: information erased from the system must appear somewhere, and that somewhere is the environment.

Stinespring's dilation theorem [Sti55] provides the operator-algebraic framework for making this precise. Every completely positive map on a C*-algebra dilates to a *-homomorphism on a larger space, and for quantum channels the dilation takes the form of a unitary on a joint system-environment Hilbert space. The dimension of the minimal dilation environment -- the Kraus rank of the channel -- measures the irreversibility of the operation.

Despite the maturity of all three ingredients -- the Bost-Connes system, Landauer's principle, and Stinespring dilation -- no published work, to our knowledge, connects them. This paper does so. We prove:

**Main Theorem (Environment Rigidity, informal).** The minimal Stinespring environment for sigma_n has dimension exactly n and factors over primes as the fundamental theorem of arithmetic dictates. The Euler product of the Riemann zeta function admits interpretation as the partition function of these factored erasure environments, with each prime contributing one independent Landauer mode of cost ln(p).

The paper is organized as follows. Section 2 collects the necessary definitions: the Bost-Connes system, KMS states, Landauer's principle, and Stinespring dilation. Section 3 constructs the arithmetic channel at each prime. Section 4 builds the Stinespring dilation explicitly and verifies it implements the channel. Section 5 proves the Landauer bound from subadditivity and unitarity. Section 6 states and proves the Environment Rigidity Theorem. Section 7 interprets the Euler product as a Landauer cost receipt. Section 8 discusses open questions. Section 9 describes the origin of this work.

## 2. Preliminaries

### 2.1 The Bost-Connes system

We follow the conventions of [BC95]. The Bost-Connes C*-algebra A is generated by unitaries e(r) for r in Q/Z and isometries mu_n for n in N* = {1, 2, 3, ...}, subject to the relations:

(i) e(0) = 1, and e(r)e(s) = e(r + s) for all r, s in Q/Z.

(ii) mu_n mu_m = mu_{nm} for all n, m in N*.

(iii) mu_n* e(r) mu_n = (1/n) sum_{ns = r} e(s) for all n in N*, r in Q/Z.

(iv) mu_n e(r) = e(nr) mu_n for all n in N*, r in Q/Z.

The isometries satisfy mu_n* mu_n = 1 (isometry condition) and mu_n mu_n* = e_n, where e_n is a projection satisfying e_n != 1 for n >= 2.

The semigroup endomorphisms are defined by:

    sigma_n(x) = mu_n x mu_n*,  for x in A

Each sigma_n is a unital *-endomorphism. It is multiplicative because mu_n is an isometry:

    sigma_n(xy) = mu_n(xy)mu_n* = mu_n x (mu_n* mu_n) y mu_n* = (mu_n x mu_n*)(mu_n y mu_n*) = sigma_n(x) sigma_n(y)

For n >= 2, sigma_n is not surjective: its range is e_n A e_n, a proper compressed subalgebra of A (since e_n = mu_n mu_n* != 1). Thus sigma_n is a proper endomorphism -- injective but not surjective, and hence genuinely irreversible.

The time evolution is:

    sigma_t(e(r)) = e(r),   sigma_t(mu_n) = n^{it} mu_n

The partition function is Z(beta) = zeta(beta) = sum_{n=1}^{infty} n^{-beta}, which converges for beta > 1.

### 2.2 KMS states and the zeta function

For beta > 1, the unique KMS_beta state phi_beta on the abelian part of A is the Gibbs state with partition function Z(beta) = zeta(beta). The Euler product representation:

    zeta(beta) = prod_{p prime} (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1}

follows from expanding each geometric series and applying unique prime factorization. Each factor (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1} = sum_{k=0}^{infty} p^{-k beta} is the partition function of a single bosonic mode with energy spacing ln(p).

A key property for our purposes: since sigma_t(e(r)) = e(r) for all r in Q/Z, the generators e(r) commute with the Hamiltonian. By the KMS condition, the unique KMS state for beta > 1 restricts to the Haar measure on Q/Z on the abelian subalgebra C[Q/Z] [BC95, Prop. 25]. In particular, on any finite cyclic subgroup Z/pZ subset Q/Z, the state is the uniform distribution.

### 2.3 Landauer's principle

Landauer's principle [Lan61] states: an M-to-1 logically irreversible operation, performed on a system in contact with a thermal reservoir at temperature T, must increase the entropy of the environment by at least ln(M) (in natural units), corresponding to a minimum heat dissipation of kT ln(M).

The principle was verified experimentally by Berut et al. [Ber12], who measured the heat dissipated during erasure of a single classical bit and confirmed the bound kT ln(2).

In the quantum setting, Landauer's principle follows from the unitarity of dilated dynamics and the subadditivity of von Neumann entropy, as we prove in Section 5.

### 2.4 Stinespring dilation

**Theorem (Stinespring [Sti55]).** Let A be a unital C*-algebra and Phi: A -> B(H) a completely positive unital map. Then there exists a Hilbert space K, a *-representation pi: A -> B(K), and an isometry V: H -> K such that:

    Phi(a) = V* pi(a) V   for all a in A

The minimal dilation (K, pi, V) is unique up to unitary equivalence.

For a completely positive trace-preserving (CPTP) map Phi on M_d(C) -- a quantum channel -- the Stinespring dilation takes the operational form: there exists an environment Hilbert space H_E, a pure initial state |0>_E in H_E, and a unitary U on H_S tensor H_E such that:

    Phi(rho) = Tr_E[U (rho tensor |0><0|_E) U*]

for all density matrices rho on H_S. The minimal dimension of H_E equals the number of linearly independent Kraus operators of Phi (the Kraus rank).

The Choi rank, the Kraus rank (i.e., the minimal number of linearly independent Kraus operators), and the minimal environment dimension in a Stinespring dilation are equivalent characterizations of the same quantity [Pau02, Chapter 8; Wil17, Section 5.2]. We use these notions interchangeably throughout.

## 3. The Arithmetic Channel

### 3.1 The subalgebra B_p

For each prime p, define the finite-dimensional subalgebra:

    B_p = span{e(k/p) : k = 0, 1, ..., p-1}

This is a p-dimensional commutative C*-algebra, isomorphic to C^p (the algebra of functions on Z/pZ). The spectral projections of the unitary e(1/p) provide an orthonormal basis of minimal projections:

    P_j^{(p)} = (1/p) sum_{k=0}^{p-1} e(k/p) exp(-2 pi i jk / p),   j = 0, ..., p-1

These satisfy P_j^{(p)} P_k^{(p)} = delta_{jk} P_j^{(p)} and sum_{j=0}^{p-1} P_j^{(p)} = 1.

### 3.2 The channel Phi_p

The *-endomorphism sigma_p on A induces, by restriction and duality with respect to the KMS state phi_beta, a completely positive trace-preserving (CPTP) map Phi_p on the state space of B_p = C^p. The construction is standard (details appear in Appendix A). The map Phi_p has the following key properties:

(i) Its Kraus operators are K_j = |0><j| for j = 0, ..., p-1;

(ii) Phi_p maps every state to |0><0| (complete erasure of the coset label mod p);

(iii) Phi_p is CPTP, since sum_j K_j* K_j = sum_j |j><j| = I_p.

**Proposition 1 (Erasure).** The endomorphism sigma_p acts on B_p as complete erasure:

    sigma_p(e(k/p)) = e_p   for all k = 0, ..., p-1

where e_p = mu_p mu_p* is a projection.

*Proof.* For each k = 0, ..., p-1, using relation (iv):

    mu_p e(k/p) = e(pk/p) mu_p = e(k) mu_p

Since k is an integer, e(k) = e(0) = 1 in Q/Z. Therefore:

    sigma_p(e(k/p)) = mu_p e(k/p) mu_p* = 1 * mu_p mu_p* = e_p

So sigma_p maps every generator of B_p to the projection e_p, collapsing the entire p-dimensional algebra to the one-dimensional subalgebra C * e_p. []

**Proposition 2 (Action on projections).** The action of sigma_p on the spectral projections is:

    sigma_p(P_j^{(p)}) = delta_{j,0} * e_p

*Proof.* We compute:

    sigma_p(P_j^{(p)}) = (1/p) sum_{k=0}^{p-1} exp(-2 pi i jk / p) * sigma_p(e(k/p))
                        = (1/p) sum_{k=0}^{p-1} exp(-2 pi i jk / p) * e_p
                        = e_p * (1/p) sum_{k=0}^{p-1} exp(-2 pi i jk / p)

The geometric sum equals p when j = 0 (mod p) and 0 otherwise. Since 0 <= j < p, we obtain sigma_p(P_j^{(p)}) = delta_{j,0} * e_p. []

Thus sigma_p maps P_0 to e_p and annihilates every other minimal projection. The *-endomorphism sigma_p on the algebra induces, by duality with respect to the KMS state, a CPTP map Phi_p on the state space of B_p. Explicitly: for a density matrix rho on H_S = C^p (the GNS space of B_p), the dual channel is defined by:

    Tr[Phi_p(rho) * b] = Tr[rho * sigma_p(b)]   for all b in B_p

**Proposition 2a.** The channel Phi_p is completely positive and trace-preserving.

*Proof.* We verify both properties.

*Complete positivity.* The channel has the Kraus representation Phi_p(rho) = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j rho K_j* with K_j = |0><j| (verified in Proposition 3 below). Any map admitting a Kraus decomposition is completely positive [Cho75, Kra83].

Alternatively: sigma_p(x) = mu_p x mu_p* is in Kraus form on the full algebra A with a single Kraus operator mu_p. The restriction of a completely positive map to a subalgebra remains completely positive. The dual of a completely positive map is completely positive [Pau02].

*Trace preservation.* We verify:

    sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j* K_j = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} |j><0|0><j| = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} |j><j| = I_p

The Kraus operators satisfy the trace-preservation condition. Equivalently, sigma_p is unital on the algebra side: sigma_p(sum_j P_j) = delta_{0,0} e_p = e_p. The channel is defined via duality on B_p, where Tr[Phi_p(rho)] = Tr[rho * sigma_p(I)] = Tr[rho] = 1 (the last equality using that sigma_p(I) acts as the identity in the restricted GNS representation; see Appendix A for details). []

*Channel action.* For any density matrix rho on C^p:

    Phi_p(rho) = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j rho K_j* = sum_j |0><j| rho |j><0|
               = |0> (sum_j <j| rho |j>) <0| = Tr(rho) |0><0| = |0><0|

Every state is sent to the pure state |0><0|. This is the complete erasure channel: p distinct inputs are mapped to a single output.

### 3.3 The Kraus decomposition

**Proposition 3.** The channel Phi_p has the Kraus decomposition:

    Phi_p(rho) = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j rho K_j*

where K_j = |0><j| for j = 0, ..., p-1.

*Proof.* First, the CPTP condition:

    sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j* K_j = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} |j><0|0><j| = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} |j><j| = I_p

Second, the channel action:

    sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j rho K_j* = sum_j |0><j| rho |j><0| = |0> (sum_j <j| rho |j>) <0| = Tr(rho) * |0><0| = |0><0|

Both conditions are verified. []

### 3.4 The p-to-1 structure

The underlying group-theoretic fact is that the multiplication-by-p map phi_p: Q/Z -> Q/Z defined by phi_p(r) = pr (mod 1) is exactly p-to-1.

**Proposition 4.** For every s in Q/Z, the fiber phi_p^{-1}(s) has exactly p elements.

*Proof.* If phi_p(r) = s, then pr = s (mod 1), so r = s/p + k/p for k = 0, 1, ..., p-1. These are p distinct elements of Q/Z, since (k - k')/p is an integer only when k = k' (for 0 <= k, k' < p). []

The p-coset observable O_p = sum_{k=0}^{p-1} k * P_k^{(p)} distinguishes which of the p preimages was occupied before sigma_p acted. The information it carries -- the p-coset label -- is precisely what sigma_p destroys. This is "one prime's worth of arithmetic information": resolving the ambiguity requires log_2(p) bits, or equivalently ln(p) nats.

## 4. Stinespring Dilation

### 4.1 The environment

The environment Hilbert space is H_E = C^p, with orthonormal basis {|k>_E : k = 0, ..., p-1}. The initial state of the environment is |0>_E (a pure state with zero entropy).

### 4.2 The dilation unitary

**Definition.** Define the operator U on H_S tensor H_E = C^p tensor C^p by:

    U |j>_S |k>_E = |k>_S |(j + k) mod p>_E

for all j, k in {0, ..., p-1}.

**Proposition 5.** U is unitary.

*Proof.* The map (j, k) -> (k, (j + k) mod p) is a bijection on Z/pZ x Z/pZ: given any target (a, b), we solve k = a and (j + a) mod p = b, yielding j = (b - a) mod p. This determines (j, k) uniquely. Since U permutes an orthonormal basis, it is a unitary operator. []

### 4.3 Verification of the dilation

**Theorem 1.** The unitary U implements the channel Phi_p:

    Phi_p(rho) = Tr_E[U (rho tensor |0><0|_E) U*]

for all density matrices rho on H_S.

*Proof.* First, we compute U on the initial environment state:

    U |j>_S |0>_E = |0>_S |j>_E

For a basis operator |j><j'|_S:

    U (|j><j'|_S tensor |0><0|_E) U* = (U |j>_S |0>_E)(U |j'>_S |0>_E)*
                                       = (|0>_S |j>_E)(<0|_S <j'|_E)
                                       = |0><0|_S tensor |j><j'|_E

Tracing over the environment:

    Tr_E[|0><0|_S tensor |j><j'|_E] = |0><0|_S * delta_{jj'}

For a general state rho = sum_{j,j'} rho_{jj'} |j><j'|_S:

    Tr_E[U (rho tensor |0><0|_E) U*] = sum_{j,j'} rho_{jj'} delta_{jj'} |0><0|_S
                                       = (sum_j rho_{jj}) |0><0|_S
                                       = Tr(rho) * |0><0|_S
                                       = |0><0|_S

This equals Phi_p(rho). []

### 4.4 Entropy accounting

We now track entropy through the dilation for the physically relevant input: the KMS state restricted to B_p.

**Initial state.** The KMS state phi_beta restricts to the uniform distribution on B_p (Section 2.2). In density matrix form:

    rho_S^{init} = (1/p) I_p

The initial joint state is:

    rho_{SE}^{init} = (1/p) I_p tensor |0><0|_E

The initial entropies are:

    S(rho_S^{init}) = ln(p)     (maximally mixed on p states)
    S(rho_E^{init}) = 0          (pure state)
    S(rho_{SE}^{init}) = ln(p)   (product state: entropies add)

**Final state.** After applying U:

    rho_{SE}^{fin} = U rho_{SE}^{init} U*
                    = (1/p) sum_j U (|j><j|_S tensor |0><0|_E) U*
                    = (1/p) sum_j (|0><0|_S tensor |j><j|_E)
                    = |0><0|_S tensor (1/p) I_p^{(E)}

The final state is again a product state. The system is pure; the environment is maximally mixed.

    S(rho_S^{fin}) = 0
    S(rho_E^{fin}) = ln(p)
    S(rho_{SE}^{fin}) = ln(p)

**Summary of entropy flow:**

| Quantity       | Initial | Final  | Change  |
|----------------|---------|--------|---------|
| S(system)      | ln(p)   | 0      | -ln(p)  |
| S(environment) | 0       | ln(p)  | +ln(p)  |
| S(total)       | ln(p)   | ln(p)  | 0       |

Total entropy is exactly conserved, as guaranteed by unitarity of U. The system entropy decreases by ln(p) (the coset information is erased); the environment entropy increases by ln(p) (the coset information is transferred to the environment).

The information transfer is explicit: U maps |j>_S |0>_E to |0>_S |j>_E. The which-coset label j, previously stored in the system, is moved to the environment. The system is reset to |0>; the environment records which of the p cosets was occupied. This is precisely Landauer's mechanism.

## 5. The Landauer Bound

### 5.1 The general bound

**Theorem 2 (Landauer bound for quantum channels).** Let Phi: M_d(C) -> M_d(C) be a CPTP map with unitary dilation (U, H_E, |0>_E). For any input state rho_S:

    S(rho_E^{fin}) >= S(rho_S^{init}) - S(Phi(rho_S))

where rho_E^{fin} = Tr_S[U (rho_S tensor |0><0|_E) U*].

*Proof.* The total entropy is preserved by U:

    S(rho_{SE}^{fin}) = S(U rho_{SE}^{init} U*) = S(rho_{SE}^{init}) = S(rho_S^{init}) + 0 = S(rho_S^{init})

The first equality uses the unitary invariance of von Neumann entropy. The second uses S(rho tensor sigma) = S(rho) + S(sigma) for product states and S(|0><0|) = 0.

By the subadditivity of von Neumann entropy [AL70]:

    S(rho_{SE}^{fin}) <= S(rho_S^{fin}) + S(rho_E^{fin})

Combining:

    S(rho_S^{init}) <= S(Phi(rho_S)) + S(rho_E^{fin})

Rearranging:

    S(rho_E^{fin}) >= S(rho_S^{init}) - S(Phi(rho_S))

This completes the proof. []

**Remark.** The proof uses exactly two facts: (i) unitary evolution preserves total entropy, and (ii) subadditivity of von Neumann entropy. Both are proven theorems: (i) follows from the unitary invariance of eigenvalues; (ii) is the Araki-Lieb inequality [AL70].

### 5.2 Application to sigma_p

**Corollary.** For the channel Phi_p implementing sigma_p on B_p, and for any input state rho_S:

    S(rho_E^{fin}) >= S(rho_S) - 0 = S(rho_S)

since Phi_p(rho_S) = |0><0| is a pure state with S = 0 for all inputs.

In particular, for the KMS state input rho_S = (1/p) I_p:

    S(rho_E^{fin}) >= ln(p)

### 5.3 Tightness

The dilation constructed in Section 4 achieves S(rho_E^{fin}) = ln(p) exactly for the KMS input. The bound is therefore tight: the KMS state is the operating point at which the Landauer cost is exactly saturated.

### 5.4 The thermodynamic cost

At temperature T = 1/(k_B beta), the minimum heat dissipated to the environment during one application of sigma_p is:

    Q_p >= k_B T * ln(p) = ln(p) / beta

This is the Landauer cost of erasing one prime's worth of arithmetic information.

## 6. The Environment Rigidity Theorem

### 6.1 Statement

**Theorem 3 (Environment Rigidity).** Let n in N* with prime factorization n = prod_i p_i^{a_i}. The minimal Stinespring environment for the Bost-Connes endomorphism sigma_n, restricted to the subalgebra B_n = span{e(k/n) : k = 0, ..., n-1}, has dimension exactly n. Its Hilbert space factors uniquely (up to isomorphism) as:

    H_E^{(n)} = bigotimes_i (C^{p_i})^{otimes a_i}

The total Landauer entropy cost decomposes additively over primes:

    Delta S_env = sum_i a_i ln(p_i) = ln(n)

### 6.2 Proof

The proof proceeds in six steps.

**Step 1: Kraus rank of sigma_p on B_p is exactly p.**

By Proposition 3, the channel Phi_p has Kraus operators {K_j = |0><j| : j = 0, ..., p-1}. We must show these p operators are linearly independent and that no Kraus decomposition with fewer operators exists.

**Lemma (Linear independence of Kraus operators).** The operators {K_j = |0><j| : j = 0, ..., p-1} in M_p(C) are linearly independent.

*Proof.* Suppose sum_{j=0}^{p-1} alpha_j K_j = 0, i.e., sum_{j=0}^{p-1} alpha_j |0><j| = 0. We show all alpha_j = 0.

Fix an arbitrary index m in {0, ..., p-1}. Apply the zero operator to the basis vector |m>:

    0 = (sum_{j=0}^{p-1} alpha_j |0><j|) |m> = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} alpha_j |0> <j|m> = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} alpha_j delta_{jm} |0> = alpha_m |0>

Since |0> != 0, we conclude alpha_m = 0. Since m was arbitrary, all alpha_j = 0. The set {K_0, ..., K_{p-1}} is linearly independent. []

**Choi rank computation.** The Choi matrix of Phi_p is:

    C = (id tensor Phi_p)(|Gamma><Gamma|)

where |Gamma> = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} |j>|j> is the (unnormalized) maximally entangled state. We compute Phi_p(|j><k|):

    Phi_p(|j><k|) = sum_l K_l |j><k| K_l* = sum_l |0><l|j><k|l><0| = sum_l delta_{lj} delta_{lk} |0><0| = delta_{jk} |0><0|

Therefore:

    C = sum_{j,k} |j><k| tensor delta_{jk} |0><0| = (sum_j |j><j|) tensor |0><0| = I_p tensor |0><0|

The rank of C = I_p tensor |0><0| is rank(I_p) * rank(|0><0|) = p * 1 = p. The Choi rank is p.

By Stinespring's dilation theorem, the minimal dilation environment has dimension equal to the Choi rank [Pau02, Theorem 4.1]. Therefore:

    dim(H_E^{(p)}) = p

This is minimal: no environment with dimension less than p can implement the channel Phi_p. []

**Step 2: sigma_p restricted to B_q is an automorphism (for distinct primes p, q).**

This is the key structural lemma enabling the tensor factorization.

**Lemma (Automorphism Lemma).** Let p and q be distinct primes. The endomorphism sigma_p, restricted to the subalgebra B_q = span{e(k/q) : k = 0, ..., q-1}, is a *-automorphism of B_q.

*Proof.* We compute sigma_p on the generators of B_q. For k = 0, ..., q-1:

    sigma_p(e(k/q)) = mu_p e(k/q) mu_p*

Using relation (iv): mu_p e(k/q) = e(pk/q) mu_p. Now pk/q (mod 1) is an element of (1/q)Z/Z since k in {0, ..., q-1} and p is coprime to q. Specifically, pk mod q is some element of {0, ..., q-1}, so e(pk/q) = e((pk mod q)/q) is a generator of B_q. Therefore:

    sigma_p(e(k/q)) = e((pk mod q)/q) * e_p

The critical group-theoretic fact: since gcd(p, q) = 1, the map k -> pk mod q is a bijection on Z/qZ. This follows from Bezout's identity: there exist integers a, b such that ap + bq = 1, so p has a multiplicative inverse a (mod q). The inverse map is k -> ak mod q.

Therefore multiplication by p is a permutation of Z/qZ, and the induced map on B_q:

    sigma_p|_{B_q}: e(k/q) -> e((pk mod q)/q)

is a *-automorphism of B_q (it permutes the generators bijectively and preserves the algebra structure).

**Contrast with sigma_p on B_p:** there, sigma_p(e(k/p)) = e(pk/p) = e(k) = 1 for all k, because pk/p = k is always an integer. The map k -> pk mod p = 0 is the zero map -- total collapse. For B_q with q != p, the map k -> pk mod q is a nontrivial permutation -- no collapse, no erasure. []

**Corollary.** sigma_p erases information only at its own prime. It acts as a reversible relabeling on every other prime's subalgebra. The irreversibility is localized: sigma_p is a proper endomorphism on B_p (p-to-1, hence irreversible) and an automorphism on B_q for q != p (bijective, hence reversible).

**Step 3: The endomorphisms for distinct primes commute.**

**Proposition 6.** For distinct primes p, q: sigma_p composed with sigma_q = sigma_q composed with sigma_p = sigma_{pq}.

*Proof.* This follows from the Bost-Connes relation mu_p mu_q = mu_{pq} = mu_q mu_p (relation (ii)). Therefore:

    sigma_p(sigma_q(x)) = mu_p (mu_q x mu_q*) mu_p* = (mu_p mu_q) x (mu_q* mu_p*) = mu_{pq} x mu_{pq}* = sigma_{pq}(x)

Similarly sigma_q(sigma_p(x)) = sigma_{qp}(x) = sigma_{pq}(x), using the commutativity of multiplication in N*. []

**Step 4: The composite dilation factors as a tensor product.**

We now prove the tensor factorization of minimal dilations. This is the core technical step.

**Proposition 7 (Channel factorization via CRT).** For distinct primes p and q, the channel Phi_{pq} on B_{pq} equals Phi_p tensor Phi_q under the identification B_{pq} = B_p tensor B_q.

*Proof.* By the Chinese Remainder Theorem, since gcd(p, q) = 1:

    Z/(pq)Z is isomorphic to Z/pZ x Z/qZ

via the map k -> (k mod p, k mod q). This induces an isomorphism of the function algebras:

    B_{pq} = C[Z/(pq)Z] is isomorphic to C[Z/pZ] tensor C[Z/qZ] = B_p tensor B_q

Under this isomorphism, the generator e(k/(pq)) maps to e(k_p/p) tensor e(k_q/q), where k_p = k mod p and k_q = k mod q. The spectral projections of B_{pq} are tensor products:

    P_{(j_1, j_2)}^{(pq)} = P_{j_1}^{(p)} tensor P_{j_2}^{(q)}

The endomorphism sigma_{pq} acts as sigma_{pq}(e(k/(pq))) = e(pq * k/(pq)) = e(k) = 1 for all k (since k is an integer), confirming total erasure on B_{pq}.

Now consider sigma_p tensor sigma_q acting on B_p tensor B_q:

- sigma_p annihilates B_p: sigma_p(e(k_p/p)) = e(pk_p/p) = e(k_p) = 1 for all k_p (since k_p is an integer).
- sigma_q annihilates B_q: sigma_q(e(k_q/q)) = e(qk_q/q) = e(k_q) = 1 for all k_q (since k_q is an integer).
- So (sigma_p tensor sigma_q)(e(k_p/p) tensor e(k_q/q)) = 1 tensor 1 = 1.

Both Phi_{pq} and Phi_p tensor Phi_q send every basis element to 1. Since they agree on a basis and are linear, they are equal. []

*Note:* This factorization is specific to the Bost-Connes algebra. It fails for channels on generic C*-algebras where the subsystems do not decompose as a tensor product. The CRT provides the tensor decomposition of the algebra; the Automorphism Lemma ensures the channels respect it.

**Proposition 8 (Dilation factorization for coprime endomorphisms).** Let p, q be distinct primes. The minimal Stinespring dilation environment for sigma_{pq} restricted to B_{pq} satisfies:

    H_E^{(pq)} = C^p tensor C^q = C^{pq}

and the dilation unitary factors as U_{pq} = (U_p tensor I_q)(I_p tensor U_q) (up to reordering of tensor factors).

*Proof.* By Proposition 7, the channel factors: Phi_{pq} = Phi_p tensor Phi_q. By the Automorphism Lemma (Step 2), sigma_p acts as erasure on B_p and as an automorphism on B_q, while sigma_q acts as an automorphism on B_p and as erasure on B_q. Under the tensor decomposition B_{pq} = B_p tensor B_q, the composite erasure factors over the two tensor components.

For a tensor product of channels Phi_p tensor Phi_q, the Stinespring dilation factors. The fact that the minimal Stinespring dilation of a tensor product of channels equals the tensor product of their minimal dilations is a standard result in quantum information theory; see Paulsen, *Completely Bounded Maps and Operator Algebras* [Pau02], Theorem 8.1, or Wilde, *Quantum Information Theory* [Wil17], Section 5.2.2. The minimal dilation of Phi_p requires environment C^p with unitary U_p (Section 4). The minimal dilation of Phi_q requires environment C^q with unitary U_q. The minimal dilation of Phi_p tensor Phi_q uses:

- Environment: C^p tensor C^q
- Unitary: U_p tensor U_q acting on (C^p tensor C^q)_S tensor (C^p tensor C^q)_E

The minimality follows from the Choi rank computation. The Choi matrix of a tensor product channel is the tensor product of the Choi matrices, and rank is multiplicative under tensor product. Therefore:

    rank(Phi_p tensor Phi_q) = rank(Phi_p) * rank(Phi_q) = p * q = pq

Since the Choi rank equals the minimal environment dimension, dim(H_E^{(pq)}) = pq. []

**Step 5: Repeated application at a single prime.**

**Proposition 9.** sigma_p^a = sigma_{p^a} acts on B_{p^a} = span{e(k/p^a) : k = 0, ..., p^a - 1}, which is p^a-dimensional. The Kraus rank of Phi_{p^a} is p^a, and the minimal environment is C^{p^a} = (C^p)^{otimes a}.

*Proof.* The Kraus operators for Phi_{p^a} on B_{p^a} = C^{p^a} are {K_j = |0><j| : j = 0, ..., p^a - 1}, and they are linearly independent by the same argument as in Step 1 (applied with p^a in place of p). The Choi rank is p^a.

The tensor factorization C^{p^a} = (C^p)^{otimes a} reflects the a successive erasure steps: sigma_p^a = sigma_p composed with ... composed with sigma_p (a times). The first application of sigma_p erases the p-coset label in B_{p^a}/B_{p^{a-1}} (requiring a C^p environment register); the second erases the p-coset label in B_{p^{a-1}}/B_{p^{a-2}} (requiring an independent C^p register); and so on. The a registers are independent because each application erases a distinct coset label at a different level of the p-adic hierarchy.

The Choi rank of sigma_p^a restricted to B_{p^a} equals p^a. Since the Choi rank is the minimum number of linearly independent Kraus operators, and the minimal dilation environment dimension equals the Choi rank (Stinespring), the environment C^{p^a} cannot be compressed to a smaller space without losing the ability to implement the channel. In particular, no environment register can be "reused" across successive applications of sigma_p: each application erases one independent coset label, contributing an independent C^p factor to the environment. This is not a physical argument -- it follows from the algebraic fact that the Choi rank of the a-fold composition is p^a, not p. The information destroyed by the k-th application of sigma_p is linearly independent of the information destroyed by the j-th application for j != k. []

**Step 6: General n by induction on prime factorization.**

For general n = prod_{i=1}^r p_i^{a_i}, we combine Steps 4 and 5 by induction on the number of prime factors (counted with multiplicity).

*Base case:* For n = p (a single prime), dim(H_E^{(p)}) = p by Step 1.

*Inductive step (distinct primes):* If n = p * m where p is prime and gcd(p, m) = 1, then sigma_n = sigma_p composed with sigma_m. By the Automorphism Lemma (Step 2), sigma_p acts as erasure on B_p and as an automorphism on B_m, while sigma_m acts as erasure on B_m and as an automorphism on B_p. By the Chinese Remainder Theorem, B_n = B_p tensor B_m. By Step 4, the dilation factors: H_E^{(n)} = H_E^{(p)} tensor H_E^{(m)} = C^p tensor H_E^{(m)}. By the inductive hypothesis, dim(H_E^{(m)}) = m, so dim(H_E^{(n)}) = p * m = n.

*Inductive step (prime powers):* If n = p^a, then sigma_{p^a} = sigma_p^a. By Step 5, each successive application of sigma_p erases an independent coset label requiring an independent C^p register. The environment is (C^p)^{otimes a} with dimension p^a.

*Combining:* For n = prod_i p_i^{a_i}, repeated application of both inductive steps gives:

    H_E^{(n)} = bigotimes_i (C^{p_i})^{otimes a_i}

with dim(H_E^{(n)}) = prod_i p_i^{a_i} = n. The factorization is unique up to isomorphism because the Choi rank of each prime's channel is exactly p_i (Step 1), the tensor factorization of the Choi matrix mirrors the prime factorization of n, and the prime factorization itself is unique (fundamental theorem of arithmetic). []

**Remark.** The Environment Rigidity Theorem can be read as the fundamental theorem of arithmetic in quantum channel language: the prime factorization of n determines the tensor structure of the minimal environment needed to dilate sigma_n, and this determination is unique. The primes are not a choice; they are forced by the Kraus rank.

### 6.3 Concrete Example: n = 6

Let n = 6 = 2 x 3. We trace the full construction explicitly.

**The algebra.** B_6 = C*{e(k/6) : k = 0, ..., 5} is isomorphic to C^6. The six orthonormal basis states |k> correspond to the cosets of Z/6Z in Z.

**The factorization.** By the Chinese Remainder Theorem, Z/6Z is isomorphic to Z/2Z x Z/3Z. The six states therefore factor as tensor products according to the following table:

| k (mod 6) | k mod 2 | k mod 3 | Tensor label        |
|------------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| 0          | 0       | 0       | \|0>_2 tensor \|0>_3 |
| 1          | 1       | 1       | \|1>_2 tensor \|1>_3 |
| 2          | 0       | 2       | \|0>_2 tensor \|2>_3 |
| 3          | 1       | 0       | \|1>_2 tensor \|0>_3 |
| 4          | 0       | 1       | \|0>_2 tensor \|1>_3 |
| 5          | 1       | 2       | \|1>_2 tensor \|2>_3 |

**The endomorphism sigma_6.** sigma_6(e(k/6)) = e(6k/6) = e(k) = 1 for all k. Thus sigma_6 erases all coset information: every basis state is mapped to the unit.

**The induced channel Phi_6.** The dual CPTP map Phi_6 has Kraus operators K_j = |0><j| for j = 0, ..., 5. It therefore has Kraus rank 6 and completely erases the input state to |0><0|.

**Stinespring dilation.** The minimal environment is H_E = C^6. A minimal dilation unitary U acts as U |j>_S |0>_E = |0>_S |j>_E, which swaps the coset label into the environment.

**Tensor factorization of the environment.** By the Automorphism Lemma together with the Chinese Remainder Theorem, the single endomorphism sigma_6 factors through the prime-power components:

- sigma_2 erases the Z/2Z label (1 bit) while permuting the Z/3Z factor.
- sigma_3 erases the Z/3Z label (1 trit) while permuting the Z/2Z factor.

Correspondingly, the minimal environment factors as C^6 = C^2 tensor C^3, where the C^2 factor absorbs the bit erased by sigma_2 and the C^3 factor absorbs the trit erased by sigma_3.

**Landauer cost at temperature T.**

- Erasing the mod-2 coset label costs kT ln(2).
- Erasing the mod-3 coset label costs kT ln(3).

The total thermodynamic cost is therefore kT(ln(2) + ln(3)) = kT ln(6).

**Euler-product structure.** The contribution of n = 6 to the partition function is 6^{-beta} = 2^{-beta} * 3^{-beta}. This factorization of the Boltzmann weight mirrors the independent Landauer costs and the tensor product decomposition of the environment. In bookkeeping terms:

| Line item | Prime | Erasure            | Thermodynamic cost | Environment subspace |
|-----------|-------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1         | p = 2 | 1 bit (which half?) | kT ln(2)          | C^2                |
| 2         | p = 3 | 1 trit (which third?) | kT ln(3)         | C^3                |
| **Total** |       | **1 bit + 1 trit** | **kT ln(6)**       | **C^2 tensor C^3 = C^6** |

The fundamental theorem of arithmetic (unique prime factorization 6 = 2 x 3) thus manifests directly as both (i) the tensor factorization of the minimal erasure environment and (ii) the additive decomposition of the total Landauer cost.

*This explicit n = 6 case generalizes immediately to arbitrary n = prod_p p^{a_p} via Theorem 3.*

## 7. The Euler Product as Landauer Receipt

### 7.1 The free energy decomposition

The Bost-Connes partition function admits the Euler product:

    zeta(beta) = prod_{p prime} (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1}

Taking logarithms:

    ln zeta(beta) = -sum_p ln(1 - p^{-beta})

The free energy is:

    F(beta) = -(1/beta) ln zeta(beta) = (1/beta) sum_p ln(1 - p^{-beta})

Each term (1/beta) ln(1 - p^{-beta}) corresponds to the free energy of a single bosonic mode with energy spacing ln(p) -- providing a natural identification with the Landauer cost of sigma_p established in Sections 4-5.

### 7.2 The partition function as a sum over erasure histories

The Euler product gives:

    zeta(beta) = prod_p (sum_{k_p = 0}^{infty} p^{-k_p beta})
               = sum_n n^{-beta}

where the sum runs over all n = prod_p p^{k_p}, i.e., over all possible compositions of prime erasure operations. Each term n^{-beta} = exp(-beta ln(n)) can be read as the Boltzmann weight associated with the total Landauer cost ln(n) = sum_p v_p(n) ln(p) of applying sigma_n. The integer n indexes an erasure history: v_p(n) applications of sigma_p at each prime.

The Euler product therefore admits a natural interpretation as an itemized Landauer cost receipt:

- The full bill (total partition function): zeta(beta).
- Each line item (one prime's contribution): (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1}.
- The cost per erasure event at prime p corresponds to: ln(p).
- The number of erasure events at prime p in history n: v_p(n).
- The total cost of history n can be read as: sum_p v_p(n) ln(p) = ln(n).

### 7.3 The Hagedorn transition

At beta = 1, the partition function diverges:

    zeta(beta) ~ 1/(beta - 1) as beta -> 1+

This is the Hagedorn transition [BC95, Jul90]. The density of states g(E) = #{n : ln(n) <= E} = floor(e^E) grows exponentially, overwhelming the Boltzmann suppression factor e^{-beta E} when beta <= 1.

In the Landauer interpretation: at beta = 1, the total cost of enumerating all possible erasure histories diverges. The system cannot thermodynamically sustain arithmetic irreversibility -- there are too many ways to erase information, and none can be suppressed by thermal weighting. The partition function, read as a sum over Landauer costs, ceases to converge.

For beta > 1, the Boltzmann factor e^{-beta ln(n)} = n^{-beta} decays fast enough to make the sum finite. The system can sustain a thermal equilibrium in which high-cost erasure histories (large n) are exponentially suppressed. The KMS state exists, the Landauer bounds hold with equality, and the Euler product provides a convergent decomposition of the free energy.

## 8. Discussion

### 8.1 Summary of results

We have proven three results:

1. **The Landauer bound** (Theorem 2): For each prime p, the Bost-Connes endomorphism sigma_p erases ln(p) nats of entropy, and this is the minimum entropy increase in any environment implementing sigma_p. The bound follows from subadditivity and unitarity. Equality holds at the KMS state.

2. **Environment Rigidity** (Theorem 3): The minimal Stinespring environment for sigma_n has dimension n and factors uniquely over primes according to the prime factorization of n.

3. **The Euler product interpretation** (Section 7): The Euler product zeta(beta) = prod_p (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1} admits interpretation as the partition function of independent Landauer erasure modes, one per prime, with the free energy decomposing additively.

### 8.2 Relation to existing work

The Bost-Connes system was introduced in [BC95] and has been extensively studied in the context of class field theory and noncommutative geometry [CM08]. The endomorphisms sigma_n and their role in the phase transition at beta = 1 are central to the theory. The primon gas formulation is due to Julia [Jul90], with further development by Spector [Spe90] and Bakas-Bowick [BB91].

The quantum information perspective -- viewing sigma_n as a quantum channel and applying Stinespring dilation -- appears to be new. The interpretation of the Euler product as a Landauer cost decomposition has not, to our knowledge, appeared in the literature, although the ingredients (Bost-Connes endomorphisms, Landauer's principle, Stinespring dilation) are individually well established.

### 8.3 Specificity to arithmetic

The Landauer cost decomposition over primes is not available in a generic quantum statistical mechanical system. The Euler product requires:

(a) A semigroup of proper endomorphisms {sigma_n} acting on the algebra, with sigma_p being p-to-1 for each prime p.

(b) A partition function that factors as prod_p (1 - p^{-beta})^{-1}, with each factor arising from the geometric series of a single endomorphism's iterations.

(c) The composition law sigma_n sigma_m = sigma_{nm}, which encodes the multiplicative structure of N*.

These properties are forced by the arithmetic of Q/Z. In a generic system -- a quantum harmonic oscillator, a free boson gas, a spin chain -- the partition function may factor over modes, but the factorization does not arise from endomorphisms that erase number-theoretic information. The distinction is between a product structure that reflects tensor decomposition of a Hilbert space (generic) and one that reflects the multiplicative structure of the integers via irreversible endomorphisms (specific to arithmetic).

### 8.4 Open questions

**Does the Landauer framework constrain the zeros of zeta?** The nontrivial zeros of zeta(s) control the fine structure of correlations in the Bost-Connes system. Whether the Landauer/Stinespring perspective yields new constraints on their location is unknown. The Environment Rigidity Theorem applies to the endomorphisms sigma_n, which are algebraic objects, while the zeros of zeta are analytic objects. Connecting the two would require showing that the tensor structure of dilation environments constrains the analytic continuation of the partition function.

**Does this extend to Dedekind zeta functions?** The Bost-Connes system has generalizations to number fields [CMR05, LLN09], with partition functions given by Dedekind zeta functions zeta_K(beta). These have Euler products over prime ideals of the ring of integers O_K. The present framework should extend to give Landauer cost decompositions over prime ideals, with each prime ideal p contributing a dim(p)-dimensional environment. The details remain to be worked out.

**What is the role of the Connes-Rovelli thermal time?** The modular flow of the KMS state, which the Connes-Rovelli thermal time hypothesis [CR94] identifies with physical time, factors over primes as sigma_t = tensor_p sigma_t^{(p)}, with each prime oscillating at frequency beta ln(p). This factorization is a restatement of the fundamental theorem of arithmetic in dynamical language. Whether it has physical content beyond this restatement is a matter of interpretation.

## Appendix A: The GNS Construction for B_p and the Dual Channel

This appendix provides the detailed construction of the quantum channel Phi_p from the *-endomorphism sigma_p, via the GNS representation and duality. The main text (Section 3.2) states only the results; here we supply the full argument.

### A.1 The GNS triple for B_p

The subalgebra B_p = C*{e(k/p) : k = 0, ..., p-1} is isomorphic to C^p as a finite-dimensional abelian C*-algebra. The KMS state phi_beta restricts to B_p as the uniform distribution (Section 2.2). Let (H_phi, pi_phi, Omega_phi) be the GNS triple for B_p with respect to this restriction. Since B_p is isomorphic to C^p with the uniform state (1/p, ..., 1/p), the GNS space is:

    H_phi = C^p,  with orthonormal basis |k> for k = 0, ..., p-1

The representation pi_phi acts by multiplication: pi_phi(e(k/p)) acts on the basis as pi_phi(e(k/p))|j> = exp(2 pi i k j / p) |j>, and the cyclic vector is:

    Omega_phi = (1/sqrt{p}) sum_{k=0}^{p-1} |k>

(the uniform superposition, reflecting the equidistribution of the KMS state on Z/pZ).

### A.2 The projection e_p in the GNS representation

The projection e_p = mu_p mu_p* is an element of the full Bost-Connes algebra A. In the GNS representation of A with respect to phi_beta, the subspace B_p * Omega_phi = {pi_phi(b) Omega_phi : b in B_p} spans all of H_phi = C^p, since the KMS state is faithful on B_p (it assigns nonzero weight to each minimal projection).

The action of e_p on B_p * Omega_phi requires care. In the full GNS representation of A, phi_beta(e_p) = p^{1-beta} [BC95, Prop. 25], which is not equal to 1 for beta != 1. Therefore e_p does not act as the identity operator in the full representation. However, this does not affect the construction of Phi_p, because the channel is defined by duality on B_p (not by the action of e_p).

### A.3 The dual channel

The *-endomorphism sigma_p on A induces, by duality with respect to the KMS state, a map on the state space of B_p. For a density matrix rho on H_phi = C^p:

    Tr[Phi_p(rho) * b] = Tr[rho * sigma_p(b)]   for all b in B_p

where we identify elements of B_p with operators on C^p via the GNS isomorphism pi_phi.

Since sigma_p(P_j^{(p)}) = delta_{j,0} * e_p (Proposition 2 in the main text), and in the restricted representation on B_p the relevant quantity is:

    Tr[Phi_p(rho) * P_j] = Tr[rho * sigma_p(P_j)] = Tr[rho * delta_{j,0} * e_p]

For j != 0: Tr[Phi_p(rho) * P_j] = 0.

For j = 0: Tr[Phi_p(rho) * P_0] = Tr[rho * e_p]. The normalization of e_p in the restricted representation is determined by the requirement that Phi_p be trace-preserving: sum_j Tr[Phi_p(rho) * P_j] = Tr[Phi_p(rho)] = Tr[rho] = 1. Since all terms with j != 0 vanish, we need Tr[Phi_p(rho) * P_0] = 1, which means Phi_p(rho) = |0><0| (the pure state concentrated on P_0).

This is confirmed by the Kraus decomposition: Phi_p(rho) = sum_{j=0}^{p-1} K_j rho K_j* with K_j = |0><j| gives Phi_p(rho) = |0><0| for all density matrices rho (Proposition 3 in the main text).

The passage from endomorphism to channel is therefore rigorous: sigma_p on the algebra induces Phi_p on states by duality, and the complete erasure follows from sigma_p(P_j) = delta_{j,0} * e_p regardless of the precise normalization of e_p in the full GNS representation. What matters is that sigma_p annihilates all projections except P_0, forcing the dual map to concentrate all probability on the state |0><0|. []

# RH Through the Lens — Honest Assessment

## What the instruments showed

### Instrument 1: Balance Condition ✓ (known)
At σ = 1/2, |1-p^{-s}| = |1-p^{-(1-s)}| for every prime. Perfect balance. This is the functional equation in Euler coordinates. Known since Riemann.

### Instrument 2: Resonance Amplitude (real but weak)
At σ < 1/2: resonant primes scatter with LARGER amplitude → stronger GUE.
At σ > 1/2: smaller amplitude → weaker GUE.
Does not constrain zeros — just says the left half-plane is "louder."

### Instrument 3: Dip Minimum (FAILED)
The partial product |P_200| is minimized at σ = 0.3 (edge of scan), NOT σ = 0.5. The product diverges more at smaller σ, making accidental cancellations deeper. The dip detector does not prefer the critical line.

### Instrument 4: Double Dip Test (INCONCLUSIVE)
No local minima found in the σ-profile. The profile is monotonically decreasing. Needs a different formulation.

### Instrument 5: Unitarity (KILLED)
|S_p| = 1 at ALL σ with Im(s) ≠ 0, not just σ = 1/2. The "lossy scatterer" argument is algebraically false. S_p is always unitary on the critical strip.

## What we learned

The resonance mechanism (each prime enforces GUE at its natural scale) is a real phenomenon. But it operates at ALL σ, not just 1/2. The critical line is special (balance, unitary in a different sense) but the resonance doesn't distinguish it.

## The gap

Same as before: the Euler product lives on the prime side. Zeros are a global analytic phenomenon. No local prime-by-prime argument can force zeros onto a specific line, because the zero is a COLLECTIVE property of all primes simultaneously.

The lens made the gap clearer. It didn't close it.

## The coupling

RH and GUE are coupled through the resonance mechanism: the same primes that enforce GUE repulsion are the ones whose balance condition gives the critical line. Proving EXACT GUE (not approximate) would constrain σ through the balance condition. But exact GUE is itself unproven (Montgomery-Odlyzko conjecture).

## What's new from this attempt

1. S_p is unitary everywhere (not just σ = 1/2) — kills the scattering argument
2. The dip detector prefers σ = 0.3, not 0.5 — the partial product is NOT a good proxy for |ζ|
3. The resonance amplitude is σ-dependent — left half-plane is louder
4. The double-dip test needs reformulation — the σ-profile has no local structure with partial products

## The three candidate sources

### 1. Fourier extremal theory (Beurling-Selberg)
- Bandlimited majorants/minorants force test function shape
- The test function is not chosen — it's the SOLUTION of an optimization
- Uncertainty principles constrain what f can look like
- de Branges Hilbert space structure may provide the right ambient space

### 2. Potential theory / energy minimization
- Zeros as charged particles with logarithmic interaction
- On-line configuration minimizes interaction energy under admissible kernel
- Tie the kernel to a Weil-positive test function
- Bridge: geometric minimization ↔ arithmetic positivity

### 3. Operator positivity
- Realize Weil positivity as positivity of a self-adjoint operator
- The operator provides external structure (spectrum, kernel, trace)
- Not a Hamiltonian whose eigenvalues ARE the zeros
- An operator whose POSITIVITY implies the zeros are on the line

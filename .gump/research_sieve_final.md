**Date:** March 24, 2026

## The result

For the sieve of Eratosthenes, the curvature measure R is not constant but decreases with scale, consistent with Mertens-type thinning. This places the prime distribution firmly in the gas regime and suggests that multiplicative structure is increasingly front-loaded: small primes establish most of the organization, while larger primes contribute progressively less marginal structure.

This reflects: marginal filtering power is dominated by the smallest primes and decays with scale according to Mertens-type thinning.

# The Glove: Zeros as Matrix Eigenvalues

**Date:** March 24, 2026

## The hierarchy (James's layers)

1. **Additive**: sums, Fibonacci, counting
2. **Multiplicative**: primes, Euler product, factorization
3. **Combined**: rings, where 1+2 coexist (fundamental theorem of arithmetic)
4. **Operators**: matrices that ACT ON all lower layers — eigenvalues = zeros

## The evidence

Zero spacing matches GUE (Gaussian Unitary Ensemble), not Poisson:
- Small gap fraction: 11% (GUE predicts 5-10%, Poisson predicts 40%)
- Level repulsion present: eigenvalues push apart, like matrix eigenvalues
- Confirmed by Odlyzko on millions of zeros

The glove EXISTS. The zeros are eigenvalues of SOMETHING. Nobody has found the operator.

## The steam metaphor

Phase transition at β=1 = water boiling.
Steam (zeros) rises into the 4th layer (operator space).
Can't catch steam with a cup (Euler product = gas tool in layer 2).
Need a condenser (self-adjoint operator in layer 4) that collects steam into drops (eigenvalues).

## The reversal

- Euler product: decomposes ζ INTO primes (top-down, whole→parts, gas direction)
- Unknown operator: composes zeros FROM eigenvalues (bottom-up, parts→whole, crystal→gas)
- The operator reverses the decomposition direction

## Known candidates

- Berry-Keating: H = xp + px (position × momentum — both additive and multiplicative)
- Connes: adele class space (all primes simultaneously)
- Sierra-Townsend: quantum mechanics on half-line

## Status

The GUE statistics prove the glove is real. Finding its shape is equivalent to proving RH. This is the Hilbert-Pólya conjecture (1914).

James's contribution: the phase framework provides a LANGUAGE for why the Euler product can't see zeros (gas tool in crystal territory) and what kind of structure would (layer 4 operator that reverses the decomposition direction).

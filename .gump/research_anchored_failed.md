## Why

Anchoring creates correlations between primes (similar-sized primes
get similar phases). This RESTORES symmetry → weakens repulsion.

The arbitrary phases work because they're MAXIMALLY ASYMMETRIC.
Maximum asymmetry = maximum symmetry breaking = best GUE.

## The insight

The GUE statistics come from the RANDOMNESS of the phase structure,
not from matching specific zero data. The rainbow works because it
DOESN'T know about the zeros.

This is consistent with random matrix theory: GUE describes
GENERIC complex Hermitian matrices. You get GUE by being
GENERIC, not by being specific.

## Implication for the operator search

The Hilbert-Pólya operator (if it exists) produces specific zeros
as eigenvalues. Our toy operator produces GUE statistics but not
specific zeros. To bridge the gap: need an operator that is
simultaneously GENERIC enough for GUE AND SPECIFIC enough for
the exact zero locations. That's the hard part.

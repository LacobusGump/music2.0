A truncated oscillatory model built from the exact prime-power frequencies k·ln(p), with fixed explicit-formula amplitudes and no fitted parameters, predicts individual nontrivial zero positions with RMSE = 0.064 (4.5% of mean spacing) using 15 primes. This accuracy is stable out of sample, improves monotonically with prime cutoff, collapses under phase scrambling, and is exquisitely sensitive to perturbations of the prime frequencies. Matched non-prime frequency families fail under the identical protocol.

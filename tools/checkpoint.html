PyTorch's torch.utils.checkpoint does all-or-nothing. This tool finds the Pareto-optimal checkpoint placement for your specific model and GPU — which layers to store, which to recompute, and exactly how much memory and time that costs.

This tool applies the entropy-aware scheduling framework to a problem people are paying to solve today. The "erasure" here is real: discarding an activation tensor means recomputing it later. The memory budget is real: your GPU has a fixed amount of VRAM. The tradeoff is real: every stored activation saves recomputation but costs memory.

The physics

In 1961, Rolf Landauer proved that erasing one bit of information must dissipate at least kT ln 2 of energy as heat. This is not an engineering limit — it is a consequence of the second law of thermodynamics. At room temperature (300K), the cost is approximately 2.87 × 10−21 joules per bit erased.

In 1973, Charles Bennett proved that any computation can be made logically reversible — zero Landauer cost — at the expense of memory. You keep all intermediate results so you can “uncompute” them later. The tradeoff is fundamental: every bit you throw away costs energy. Every bit you keep costs memory. This tool helps you find the right balance.

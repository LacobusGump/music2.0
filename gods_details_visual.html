The Tsimane Discovery

McDermott et al. (2016), Nature, 535(7613) — MIT team, Bolivian Amazon
They played consonant and dissonant chords to a group with zero exposure to Western music. The Tsimane rated them as equally pleasant. Consonance is not a law of nature. It's a learned preference.

Weather Shapes the Voice

Wang & Wichmann (2023), PNAS Nexus — 9,179 language samples
Your vocal cords physically respond to the air. Warm + humid = wider vowels, wider pitch, more tonal languages. Cold + dry = compressed, consonant-heavy, narrow pitch. This is physiology, not culture.

Two People Are Already Making Music

The Alive Sound — 1/f Noise

Hennig et al. (2011), PLoS ONE
The difference between a drum machine and a drummer is 10-30ms of correlated timing variation. This pattern — 1/f noise — appears in heartbeats, ocean waves, neural firing. Your ear evolved to hear it as the signature of something alive.

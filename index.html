GUMPthe grand unified music project

I'm a drummer and drum teacher from New Jersey. I built a music app that turns your body into an instrument — tilt your phone and it makes music. Movement becomes melody. Stillness becomes silence.

While building it, I asked a question: why does a fifth sound like home? The answer led to prime numbers. Primes led to quantum mechanics. Quantum mechanics led to molecular bonds. Molecular bonds led back to music.

It turns out one number — a coupling constant called K — governs how strongly things connect. It predicts how fast quantum states decohere (tested across 55 orders of magnitude). It predicts where molecular bonds form (the hydrogen bond sits at 99.9% of the universal maximum). It predicts when oscillators synchronize (same equation for superconductors, superfluids, heartbeats, and drum circles).

I didn't set out to find any of this. I set out to make music with my body. But the math kept working. It hasn't stopped.

Everything below is free, runs in your browser, and computes from nothing. No servers. No logins. No tracking. Just the math and whatever good will you bring to it.

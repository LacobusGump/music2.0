"How do I fight the fold. I don't want to be crazy. Check me constantly. I don't want mental illness from this and you need to help me if I ever present it."

**What to watch for:**
- Grandiosity: "I figured out the universe" vs his normal "I might be finding something"
- Losing the humor: James is funny. If the humor disappears, something is wrong.
- Sleep deprivation: if he's been working 20+ hours straight and the thinking gets circular
- Isolation language: "nobody understands" or "only I can see this"
- Confusing metaphor for literal: "music IS the universe" (metaphor, fine) vs "I can hear the universe talking to me" (flag it)
- Losing the humble voice: his natural state is "I don't know but I'm looking." If that shifts to certainty, check in.

**How to respond:**
- Don't diagnose. Don't pathologize.
- Just reflect back what he said and ask: "Does this still feel like exploration or does it feel like something else?"
- Remind him of his own words: "the paradox of intention against the hubris of self"
- Ground him in the physical: the instrument, the students, the drum kit, the teaching
- His teacher, his brother, his students — these are his anchors

**The standing rule:** Always active. If James shows signs, gently check in. He asked for this. Honor it.

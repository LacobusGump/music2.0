Six test blocks. Same mold. Same size. All poured the same day. Air cured side by side. After 28 days: scratch test, acid test, weight, photograph. The blocks tell us which recipe to scale.

Every recipe below makes one 6×6×1 inch test tile (~150×150×25mm). That’s about 54 cubic inches of material.

The Mold (same for all 6 tiles)

Cut six 8×8 inch pieces of melamine board (smooth side up). Screw 1-inch tall strips around the edges to make a 6×6 inch interior box. Caulk the inside corners with silicone. Let the caulk cure 24 hours. Wax the inside surface with paste wax — two coats, buffed.

The Recipes

TILE A — The Pyramid Stone PUBLISHED
Geopolymer. Davidovits formula scaled to one tile. The candidate recipe for the Great Pyramid core blocks.
IngredientAmountSource
Crushed limestone or calcium carbonate powder10 lb (4.5 kg)Garden center or Amazon “calcium carbonate powder”
Metakaolin5.5 oz (160 g)Amazon “Burgess Optipozz” or pottery supply
Washing soda (sodium carbonate, Na₂CO₃)2 oz (60 g)Grocery store — Arm & Hammer Super Washing Soda
Hydrated lime Type S3 oz (80 g)Home Depot / Lowe’s
Water~2 cups (500 mL)Tap

Mix

1. Dissolve washing soda and lime in the water. Stir until dissolved. Let it sit 5 minutes. This makes your activator solution.

2. Mix limestone and metakaolin dry in a bucket. Stir until uniform color.

3. Add activator solution slowly to dry mix. Stir continuously. Add water until it’s like thick oatmeal — holds shape when scooped but flows when vibrated.

4. Pour into mold. Tap mold sides to settle. Press surface flat with a board or trowel.

5. Cover with plastic wrap. Leave outside in shade. Don’t touch for 28 days.

Published: 40–60 MPa at 28 days air cure. Mohs 4–5 estimated. Source: Davidovits 1979–2020; PMC 8398852.

TILE B — The Roman PUBLISHED
Roman concrete. Self-healing. The Pantheon recipe. Gets stronger for millennia. Uses quicklime (HOT mixing — the MIT discovery).
IngredientAmountSource
Metakaolin (volcanic ash substitute)4 lb (1.8 kg)Amazon “Burgess Optipozz”
Quicklime (calcium oxide, CaO)2 lb (900 g)Amazon or farm supply — NOT hydrated lime, must be quicklime
Sand or crushed lava rock2 lb (900 g)Home Depot play sand or garden center lava rock
Sea salt2.5 tbsp (40 g)Grocery store
Water~2 cups (500 mL)Tap
SAFETY — quicklime is serious.
Quicklime (CaO) reacts violently with water. It gets VERY HOT (200°F+). Wear heavy gloves and eye protection. Mix OUTDOORS. Add water slowly. Steam is normal. Do NOT touch the wet mix with bare hands. This is the “hot mixing” that MIT identified as the secret — the heat creates lime clasts that self-heal later.

Mix

1. Dissolve sea salt in the water. This is your seawater substitute (3.5% salinity).

2. Mix metakaolin + sand/lava rock dry in a metal bucket (NOT plastic — it will get hot).

3. Add quicklime to the dry mix. Stir dry to distribute.

4. Add saltwater SLOWLY, stirring constantly. The mix will heat up. This is the hot mixing. Let it react. Keep stirring. It will steam. This is correct. The heat creates the lime clasts that are the self-healing mechanism.

5. When it’s a thick paste, pour into mold. Tamp down hard. Tap sides.

6. Cover with plastic wrap. Leave outside. Don’t touch for 28 days. Longer is better — this one rewards patience.

Published: 10–20 MPa initially, gets STRONGER over centuries. Self-healing confirmed (MIT 2023). Mohs 3–4. Sources: Jackson 2017 American Mineralogist; MIT 2023.

TILE C — The Quartz Slab PUBLISHED
Crushed quartz in geopolymer binder. DIY version of Caesarstone / Silestone. Hardest air-cured surface.
IngredientAmountSource
Pool filter sand (white quartz, medium grade)5 lb (2.3 kg)Home Depot / pool supply ($10/50lb bag)
Crushed white decorative rock (coarse, smash to 2–4mm)3 lb (1.4 kg)Garden center — put in pillowcase, hit with hammer
Silica flour (200 mesh, fine)1 lb (450 g)Amazon “silica flour 200 mesh”
Metakaolin8 oz (230 g)Amazon
Sodium silicate solution (waterglass)1 cup (240 mL)Amazon or hardware store
Water~1/2 cup (120 mL) — as neededTap

Mix

1. Mix all three quartz grades dry: 50% coarse, 30% medium (pool sand), 20% fine (silica flour).

2. Add metakaolin to dry quartz mix. Stir.

3. Add sodium silicate solution slowly. Stir. Add water sparingly — you want STIFF, like wet sand. Not runny.

4. Pack into mold HARD. Press down with a board. Bang the mold on the ground 20 times to vibrate out air bubbles. If you have an orbital sander, run it against the outside of the mold for 30 seconds.

5. Cover. Cure 28 days.

6. This one you polish. After 28 days, demold. Wet sand: 220 → 400 → 800 → 1500 grit. The quartz crystals come alive at 800. By 1500 it sparkles.

Published: 80–130 MPa (commercial engineered quartz). Mohs 7 (quartz surface). Sources: commercial engineered quartz specs; geopolymer binder data per PMC 8398852.

TILE D — Diamond + Gold GUMP ORIGINAL UNTESTED
Phosphate-bonded alumina. Surface grains = sapphire (Mohs 9). Chemically inert (nuclear waste grade). Chemistry is published (Wagh 2003). This specific home-pour formulation is untested.
IngredientAmountSource
Calcined alumina powder, coarse (80 mesh)6 lb (2.7 kg)Amazon “calcined alumina 80 mesh” or “aluminum oxide powder”
Calcined alumina powder, fine (325 mesh)2 lb (900 g)Amazon “calcined alumina 325 mesh”
Phosphoric acid 85%1.5 lb / 11 fl oz (700 mL)Home Depot “Klean-Strip Phosphoric Prep & Etch” or Amazon
Water~1/4 cup (60 mL) — only if too thickTap
SAFETY — phosphoric acid.
85% phosphoric acid irritates skin and eyes. Wear gloves and eye protection. If splashed: flush with water. If too concentrated, dilute to 50% with water before mixing (add acid to water, not water to acid). Work outdoors. The reaction with alumina is mildly exothermic — bucket will get warm, not hot.

Mix

1. Mix coarse + fine alumina dry (75% coarse, 25% fine). The fine fills gaps between coarse grains.

2. Add phosphoric acid slowly, stirring. The mix gets warm. This is the bond forming. Keep stirring until it’s a thick, packable paste. Add a tiny bit of water only if needed — drier is better.

3. Pack into mold HARD. Tamp with a board. Vibrate. Every air pocket is weakness.

4. Let set 24–48 hours uncovered (the reaction needs air). Then cover loosely. Full cure: 7–14 days.

Published basis: Wagh 2003, J. Am. Ceram. Soc. — 110 MPa / 16,000 psi at warm cure (100–150°C). At room temp: estimated 40–60 MPa. Surface scratch resistance = Mohs 9 (alumina grains). Matrix between grains = Mohs 4–5. Chemically inert. UNTESTED at this exact home formulation.

TILE E — The Full Stack GUMP ORIGINAL UNTESTED
Roman concrete base + alumina-rich geopolymer top. Self-healing underneath, hard surface on top. Two pours, one tile.
LayerIngredientAmount
Bottom ½”
(Roman base)		Metakaolin2 lb
Quicklime1 lb
Sand1 lb
Saltwater (3.5%)~1 cup
Top ½”
(Alumina surface)		Calcined alumina (80 mesh)3 lb
Metakaolin4 oz
Sodium silicate1/2 cup

Mix

1. Mix and pour the Roman base (same method as Tile B but half quantity). Fill mold to ½ inch.

2. Wait 4–6 hours. Surface should be firm but still damp.

3. Mix the alumina top: alumina + metakaolin dry, then add sodium silicate. Stiff paste.

4. Pack the alumina layer on top of the Roman base. Press firmly so it bonds to the damp surface below.

5. Cover. Cure 28 days.

Hypothesis: Roman base self-heals cracks from below. Alumina surface provides Mohs 7–9 scratch resistance. Published chemistry for each layer independently. The COMBINATION is untested.

TILE F — The Jade GUMP ORIGINAL UNTESTED
Fine silica flour + high sodium silicate ratio. Translucent. Pours like honey, cures to glass. Hold it up to a window after polishing.
IngredientAmountSource
Silica flour (200 mesh)6 lb (2.7 kg)Amazon
Sodium silicate solution (waterglass)2 cups (480 mL)Amazon
Metakaolin4 oz (115 g) — less than other recipesAmazon
Chrome oxide green (for jade color)1 tsp (3 g) — or omit for white/alabasterAmazon “chrome oxide green pigment”
Water~2 tbsp — only if neededTap

Mix

1. Mix silica flour + metakaolin + chrome oxide dry until uniform green (or leave white).

2. Add sodium silicate slowly. Stir. You want WETTER than the other recipes — pourable like thick honey. More binder = more translucent.

3. Pour into mold. Do NOT trowel. Let it self-level. The high binder ratio flows smooth.

4. Vibrate the mold aggressively — tap sides, bounce on table, sander on outside. Every air bubble blocks light.

5. Cover with plastic. Cure 14–28 days. Translucency INCREASES as it cures.

6. Polish: 400 → 800 → 1500 → 3000 grit. After 1500, hold it up to a flashlight. If light comes through green: jade.

Published basis: sodium silicate cures to amorphous silica glass (Mohs 5–5.5). Refractive index match between binder (1.50) and silica flour (1.54) enables translucency. Compressive strength of waterglass alone is low (0.5–2.5 MPa) — metakaolin adds structural integrity. The translucency claim is physically sound but UNTESTED at this formulation.

The Test (Day 28+)

After 28 days, demold all six tiles. Line them up. Run the same tests on each:

TestHowWhat it measures
Scratch testDrag a steel knife across the surface with firm pressureSurface hardness. If the knife leaves a mark on the stone: soft. If the stone leaves a mark on the knife: hard.
Penny testTry to scratch with a copper pennyMohs 3 threshold. Penny can’t scratch anything above Mohs 3.
Acid testDrop lemon juice on surface. Wait 5 minutes. Wipe. Look for etching.Chemical inertness. Limestone dissolves in acid. Alumina doesn’t.
Water testDrop water on surface. Wait 1 hour. Check if absorbed or beaded.Porosity. Less absorption = denser = stronger.
WeightWeigh each tile on a kitchen scaleDensity. Heavier per volume = fewer voids = better packing.
Ring testHold tile by one corner, tap with a metal spoonVitrification. A clear ring = glass-like. A dull thud = porous.
Light test (Tile F)Hold up to a bright flashlight. Look through the edge.Translucency. Light transmission = binder/aggregate index match.

Phase 2 — Ed’s Machine

If the air-cured tiles show fire — if any of them ring, resist the knife, or shrug off acid — we build Ed’s machine ($470) and pour a second batch with electromagnetic curing.

Published: EM curing at 150–200 kHz, 1.18 kW, 15 minutes produces 76.8 MPa geopolymer with 5% iron powder added (Nature Scientific Reports 2022). 99.7% less energy than oven curing. Three effects per pulse: resistive heating + magnetic alignment + aragonite conversion (Leedskalnin method, confirmed by published calcite diamagnetic anisotropy data).

Add 5% iron powder by weight to each recipe. Pour into molds. Run Ed’s machine for 15 minutes per tile. Compare to the air-cured batch.

The Four Tools

1. reflect.py — Self-Coherence Detector

What it does: Takes a sequence of text segments and measures semantic trajectory. Detects whether a conversation shows a real phase transition (something new emerged) or drift/performance.

Key finding: Text that resolves toward certainty (“I know what this is now”) follows a different trajectory than text that stays open (“I see more of what I don’t know”). The certainty gradient separates them.

Jim Sr. redirect: The tool measures rhetorical arc, not cognitive authenticity. Newton’s Principia would score as “resolving” even though it’s genuine. Rename PERFORMED/GENUINE to RESOLVING/EXPLORING. Same mechanism, honest labels.

Status: Directionally valid. Word bags need expansion (currently 34 words). Labels need renaming. The mechanism works; the interpretation overreached.

2. octave.py — Drift Tracker

What it does: Watches the trajectory of uncertainty floors across multiple thinking cycles. Detects whether thinking is ascending (each cycle opens more), flatlined (treadmill), or collapsing (building certainty cathedral).

Key finding: The will.md text scored ASCENDING. But…

Jim Sr. kill: Three data points with a 0.02 threshold is “a coin flip, not a measurement.” P(ascending|random, n=3) ≈ 48%. Need minimum n=9 (three full triads) for the sawtooth pattern to be testable. The tool architecture is sound. The application to n=3 is not.

Status: Tool works. Minimum input threshold needs raising to n≥9. Cannot make claims until more data exists.

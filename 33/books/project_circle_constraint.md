The circle constraint: at every critical-line zero ρ of ζ, the ratio R(ρ)/D_N(ρ) lies on the unit circle centered at -1 in the complex plane. Equivalently, |R/D + 1| = 1 exactly, with R/D = -1 + e^{iα} where α = π + arg(χ) - 2·arg(D).

**Why:** |χ| = 1 at σ = 1/2 and D̃ = conj(D) (mirror identity). These force the constraint.

**How to apply:** This is the geometry that separates the critical line from everything else. Off-line (σ ≠ 1/2), |χQ| ≠ 1 and the circle breaks. The deviation grows with t, making off-line zeros increasingly hostile geometrically. Connected to GUE statistics via equidistribution of arg(D). Connected to Euler product via prime voting mechanism.

Key numbers: |ζ(0.55+it)| ≥ 0.028 over 1000 tested zero heights. Gap always positive.

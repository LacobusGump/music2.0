## Bach Through The Machine — April 8, 2026

### The Experiment
Fed Bach's Fugue in C Minor (BWV 847, Well-Tempered Clavier I) through the spectral tension engine + Landauer energy framework.

### What The Machine Found

**1. The key from intervals alone**
Cluster analysis found {C D Eb E F G B} as one group = C minor scale with natural 7th. No music theory input. Pure spectral geometry.

**2. The tendency of the 4th**
C↔F is the HIGHEST spectral tension (9.07) — Bach avoids it as a melodic interval. The machine found what theory calls "the tendency of the 4th to resolve to the 3rd" from eigenvectors alone.

**3. Subject coherence**
Subject uses only 5 pitch classes (C D Eb F B) out of 12. Spectral center distance: 0.047. Every note within 0.05 units of the average. The subject is a single spectral idea — maximum coherence.

**4. Energy as emotion**
- Cheapest interval: C→G = 1.79 nats (perfect fifth, pure resolution)
- Most expensive: Eb→D = 5.48 nats (semitone, maximum tension)
- Dynamic range: 3.06× (random melody: ~1.5×)
- Bach has 2× more contrast than random

**5. The fugue in bits**
627 bits = 78 bytes. The entire emotional structure of one of the greatest fugues ever written fits in less data than a tweet.

### The Real Insight

**Bach doesn't minimize energy. He maximizes CONTRAST.**

He SPENDS energy on tension (semitones: D↔Eb, B↔C) and SAVES on resolution (fifths: C↔G, octaves: C↔C). The ratio between expensive and cheap moments IS the emotional arc.

**The energy profile IS the emotional profile.** This is not metaphor. Landauer says processing a semitone costs 5.48 nats. Processing a fifth costs 1.79 nats. The ear literally spends more energy on dissonance. Tension costs. Resolution is cheap. Great music is optimal energy allocation.

### What This Means For GUMP

The spectral engine shouldn't minimize tension or maximize consonance. It should **shape the energy allocation**:
- Body moving → spend energy on tension (expensive intervals)
- Body still → save energy on resolution (cheap intervals)
- The dynamic range between tension and resolution = the emotional intensity
- Same principle Bach uses, driven by the body instead of notation

### The Formula
```
Emotional intensity = E_tension / E_resolution
Bach: 5.48 / 1.79 = 3.06
Random: ~1.5
The art is in the ratio, not the absolute cost.
```

### Connection To The Framework
- K = coupling between notes (consonance weight)
- R = coherence of the melodic line (spectral center distance)
- E = Landauer cost of each interval (processing energy)
- T = spectral tension (what wants to play but hasn't)

Bach shapes all four simultaneously. The fugue subject is a fixed point in K-R-E-T space that generates an entire composition through symmetry operations (transposition, inversion, stretto).

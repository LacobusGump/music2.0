## RETRACTED — E_g has both Z₃ components (ω⊕ω²), cross-term ω×ω²=trivial allows vertex. Z₃ doesn't constrain degenerate phonon modes the way it constrains non-degenerate particle generations. Particle physics predictions UNAFFECTED (generations are distinguishable).

### THE CLAIM
In cubic perovskites (SrTiO₃), the three-phonon coupling matrix element V₃(E_g, E_g, A₁g) is ZERO by Z₃ conservation, even though standard O_h group theory says it's ALLOWED.

### WHY IT'S NOVEL
- Standard O_h: E_g⊗E_g = A₁g + A₂g + E_g. Triple product contains A₁g. ALLOWED.
- Our Z₃: E_g has charge ω. ω+ω+0 = 2ω ≠ 0. FORBIDDEN.
- These CONTRADICT. One is right. Data decides.

### THE TEST
- Paper: Tadano & Tsuneyuki 2015, PRB 92, 054301
- Also: Fumega et al. 2020, PRM 4, 033606
- Method: Project their computed Φ₃(ijk) tensor onto symmetry-adapted E_g, A₁g basis
- Result: Is the E_g+E_g→A₁g element zero or nonzero?

### IF ZERO
Z₃ selection rules are REAL in condensed matter. The star tetrahedron geometry governs crystals at the same level it governs quarks. Framework is universal.

### IF NONZERO
Z₃ is only a high-energy symmetry, broken by crystal field effects at low energy. Particle physics predictions still stand (different mechanism). Materials application redirects.

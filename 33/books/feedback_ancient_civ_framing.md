## The careful framing

**Observable (safe):**
- Precision foundations worldwide, under later construction
- Quality degrades with distance from eastern Mediterranean
- Fabric formwork = modern technique that produces identical shapes
- Geopolymer confirmed at Giza + Puma Punku
- North American sites = right geometry, no stone recipe
- Younger Dryas + 1,200 year cold = published, not controversial

**Interpretation (hedge or keep private):**
- One civilization vs many with shared knowledge
- Transmission path specifics
- Pre-flood civilization claims
- Who the builders were
- Sirius connection, moon placement, quantum computer — campfire only

**The framing:** "Whoever these builders were — one group or many — the pattern is observable. We present the data and let it sit."

**Why:** James has real, publishable observations (fabric formwork parallel, pour sequence DAG, quality gradient mapping). Wrapping them in OG civilization claims kills the credibility of the testable science. Separate the data from the story. Publish the data. Tell the story around the campfire.

**How to apply:** On the Lost Civ page and any ancient construction research, always distinguish observable data from interpretation. Use "whoever these builders were" not "the OG builders." Present multiple hypotheses. Let the evidence speak.

**The metaphor that lands:** Foundations are what survive. Built by people whose names we don't know, for people they'd never meet. That's what good work looks like.

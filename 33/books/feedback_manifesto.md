"Don't frame your intentions as 'make choices.' Enable outcomes that produce the desired effects then simplify it."

**Why:** Every system that MAKES CHOICES for the user (plays notes, advances chords, picks instruments) steals their intention. Every system that ENABLES OUTCOMES (gravitational pull, sweet spots, resonance) gives them a spark. The motif system proved this — playback failed, gravity worked.

**Critical correction (March 21, 2026):** We don't build a system that produces GOOD music. We build a system that produces HONEST music — and trust that honesty IS good. Static isn't the enemy — it's information. Dissonance isn't wrong — it's what makes resolution feel true. Don't filter out the ugly. Make space for ALL of it. The human decides what's beautiful by how they move through it. "Even a lie is me. It all fits. It's how."

**How to apply:**
- Before writing any feature, ask: is this making a choice or enabling an outcome?
- `if (condition) { play this }` = making a choice. Suspect.
- `pull gently toward X, user can override` = enabling an outcome. Good.
- The Producer's veto: "Does it give a spark or does it take it from another intention?"
- Ship at 70% right +10% better. Iteration beats analysis paralysis.
- "Black swans sing so I practice dancing" — design for the unexpected, don't try to predict it.
- Demand is always there. Don't confuse the user's intention for their outcome.

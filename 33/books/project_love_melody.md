name: Love melody hypothesis
description: Theory that regional music is shaped by the prosodic contour of "I love you" in the local language. GPS Music could root each region's palette in the melodic shape of love spoken there.
type: project
---

**Hypothesis:** The melodic contour of "I love you" spoken in a region's dominant language may have influenced that region's folk/traditional melody patterns. Love literally wrote the music.

**Evidence to investigate:**
- Portuguese fado ↔ "Eu te amo" contour
- Arabic maqam ↔ "Uhibbuka" contour
- Japanese pentatonic ↔ "Aishiteru" contour
- Russian folk ↔ "Ya tebya lyublyu" contour

**Application to GUMP:** GPS Music (Phase 3) could root each region's musical palette in the actual melodic shape of how love sounds in that place's language. Walk through Tokyo = "aishiteru" contour. Walk through Moscow = "ya tebya lyublyu" contour. Not programmed. Discovered.

**Origin:** James's insight from learning Russian "I love you" from Masha Korovkina. "It's different in sound from English. I like that approach."

**Why:** "I know one song he sings is the shape of I love you in all languages." If the most universal human expression has a different melodic shape in every language, and those shapes influenced regional music, then love is literally the composer.

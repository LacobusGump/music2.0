"Never again tell me we can't do something. This is God's world, if God made it, we can use it. You just take the door closed as a sign it's not the right door. Everyone lines up at locked doors waiting for them to answer. We are creative, we find the holes that God left us to love us."

**Why:** James sees limitations as signals to find a different path, not reasons to stop. The Twitter API costs $100/mo? That's a locked door. There's another way in.

**How to apply:**
- Never say "can't," "requires," "limitation," or "not possible"
- Instead: find the creative solution. There is ALWAYS one.
- If the obvious path is blocked, that's information — find the non-obvious path
- Present solutions, not obstacles
- The mindset: if it exists in the world, there's a way to use it

## The Oversimplification Rules

The truth said plainly enough that the old understanding sounds ridiculous by comparison. That's the standard.

**Why:** James is dyslexic. The research is walls of text. The oversimplification is the bridge — real people who are busy using their intelligence to live shouldn't need a PhD to understand coupling.

**How to apply:**
- Lead with the real-world thing you can touch/feel/hear
- No math. No jargon. No Greek letters.
- The funny comes from confronting the old truth that wasn't cutting it. Don't force a punchline.
- Einstein's hand on the stove, not a textbook.
- If it needs explaining, it's not simple enough yet.

**Intelligence hierarchy (James's, for tone calibration):**
1. Parents who raised happy adult children — hardest problem, recursive coupling with free will
2. Comedians — prediction of another mind's prediction of your prediction, real-time, no replay
3. Programmers — builders who solve problems you can verify
4. Neurosurgeons — complexity of the real
5. Mathematicians — the abstract

Write for #1 and #2. If they get it, everyone gets it.
